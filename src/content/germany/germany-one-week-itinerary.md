Seven days is enough to see the essential Germany — Munich's Altstadt and beer culture, a castle day trip, a scenic Bavarian village, and Berlin's history and energy. This itinerary splits 3 days in Munich and 4 days in Berlin, connected by a 4-hour ICE train.

**Best time**: May–September for outdoor dining and beer gardens; December for Christmas markets; Oktoberfest (September 19 – October 4) adds a festival layer but requires booking accommodation 6+ months ahead.

---

## Day 1 — Munich: Altstadt and First Beer Garden

**Morning**: Arrive at Munich Hauptbahnhof. Check in (book a hotel within walking distance of Marienplatz — the Altstadt is the right base for days 1–3).

**10:30 AM**: Walk to **Marienplatz** — the Glockenspiel performs at 11 AM (15 minutes, free). Examine the Neues Rathaus facade. Walk through to **Viktualienmarkt** immediately south: 140 stalls of Bavarian produce, a beer garden in the center, and some of the best Weißwurst in Munich at the market butcher stalls.

**Noon**: Lunch at Viktualienmarkt beer garden — Maß of local beer, Obatzda (Bavarian cheese spread) with pretzel, and whatever the market's food stalls are serving.

**Afternoon**: Walk east to **Hofbräuhaus** (15 minutes from Marienplatz) — the world's most famous beer hall. Walk through, have a look at the ground floor vaulted room, decide whether you want a midday beer. Then continue to the **Residenz** (the Wittelsbach royal palace, the largest city palace in Germany) — entry €9, 2–3 hours for the main rooms.

**Evening**: **Augustiner Keller** beer garden on Arnulfstraße — the most beloved beer garden by Munich locals, 5,000 seats under chestnut trees, Augustiner served from wooden barrels. Dinner here: Schweinsbraten (roast pork), Semmelknödel (bread dumplings), and a proper Maß.

## Day 2 — Neuschwanstein and Alpine Foothills

A full-day excursion to Bavaria's most iconic landmark.

**7:30 AM**: Train from Munich Hauptbahnhof to **Füssen** (2 hours, ~€30 with regional pass or Sparpreis). *Book Neuschwanstein tickets in advance at hohenschwangau.de* — they sell out months ahead in summer.

**10:00 AM**: Bus or taxi from Füssen station to Hohenschwangau village (10 minutes). Walk up to **Neuschwanstein Castle** — the 19th-century fairy-tale castle commissioned by Ludwig II, perched above a gorge with the Alps behind it. Guided tour lasts 35 minutes. The Marienbrücke bridge (15 minutes' walk above the castle) gives the classic postcard view.

**1:00 PM**: Lunch in Füssen or the village — traditional Bavarian restaurant, €15–25/person.

**Afternoon**: Optional: Hohenschwangau Castle (adjacent, the yellow castle where Ludwig II grew up, €20 entry) or walk the Alpsee lakeside trail (free, 45 minutes). Return to Munich by 8 PM.

**Evening**: Quieter dinner in Munich — try **Wirtshaus in der Au** in the Haidhausen neighborhood for traditional Bavarian food at non-tourist prices.

---

## Day 3 — Munich: Museums and English Garden

**Morning**: **Deutsches Museum** — the world's largest science and technology museum. Entry €16, open 9 AM. Focus on 2–3 departments rather than trying to see everything: aviation and aerospace, the marine navigation hall, or the mine simulation (underground). Allow 3–4 hours minimum.

**1:00 PM**: Walk across the Isar to the **English Garden**. Lunch at the **Chinesischer Turm** beer garden (7,000 seats, self-service, bring your own food or buy from the stalls).

**Afternoon**: Walk through the park to the **Eisbach wave** at the southern end — watch the river surfers from the Prinzregentenstraße bridge. Then walk or tram to the **Pinakothek der Moderne** (modern art, design, architecture; €10, Sunday €1) for 2 hours.

**Evening**: Train to Berlin. The 6 PM ICE arrives in Berlin by 10 PM (4 hours, ~€50 Sparpreis booked ahead). Check in to Berlin hotel.

*Alternative if you're not going to Berlin: Use day 3 for Nymphenburg Palace + grounds (Schloss Nymphenburg, €8 entry, 200 hectares of gardens), the Alte Pinakothek (Old Masters, €7), or a second beer garden afternoon.*

## Day 4 — Berlin: Cold War History

Berlin is a city that can't be understood without its 20th-century history. Start here.

**Morning**: **Berliner Mauer Dokumentationszentrum** (Berlin Wall Memorial) on Bernauer Straße — free, open Tuesday–Sunday. The outdoor memorial preserves an original stretch of the wall with the death strip; the documentation center explains the division of Germany 1961–1989 with photographs and personal accounts. 2 hours.

**Noon**: Walk or U-Bahn to **Checkpoint Charlie** — the famous crossing point between East and West Berlin. The museum (€17) is tourist-heavy but has extraordinary primary sources. The outdoor markers showing where the wall stood are free.

**Afternoon**: **Topography of Terror** (free) — an outdoor and indoor documentation center on the site of the former SS and Gestapo headquarters. The permanent exhibition is one of the most important in Berlin on the Nazi period.

**Evening**: Walk through **Kreuzberg** for dinner — Berlin's most multicultural neighborhood, with excellent Turkish, Vietnamese, and international restaurants at reasonable prices. The area around Oranienstraße is particularly good.

---

## Day 5 — Berlin: Cold War Part II + Brandenburg Gate

**Morning**: **East Side Gallery** — the longest remaining section of the Berlin Wall (1.3 km), painted by artists from 21 countries in 1990. Walk the full stretch along the Spree River. Free, outdoor, best in morning light.

**Noon**: U-Bahn to **Museumsinsel** (Museum Island) — a UNESCO World Heritage Site with 5 major museums. Pick one: the **Pergamon Museum** (Hellenistic altar, Ishtar Gate from Babylon, Market Gate of Miletus — genuinely extraordinary ancient architecture reassembled in the museum, €12) or the **Neues Museum** (Egyptian collection including the famous Nefertiti bust, €12).

**Afternoon**: Walk west through the Lustgarten to **Unter den Linden** boulevard, past the **Humboldt University** and **Neue Wache**, to the **Brandenburg Gate** — the most recognizable symbol in Berlin. Free, always open. Walk through to the Pariser Platz side.

**5:00 PM**: **Reichstag building** — free entry to the glass dome, but book weeks ahead at bundestag.de (the dome closes when Parliament is in session). 360-degree views of Berlin's skyline at sunset.

**Evening**: Dinner in **Mitte** or **Prenzlauer Berg** — the latter is the neighborhood for a relaxed dinner at mid-range prices (€20–35/person).

---

## Day 6 — Berlin: Neighborhoods and Contemporary Culture

**Morning**: **Prenzlauer Berg** on foot — the best-preserved 19th-century residential neighborhood in Berlin, with the **Mauerpark** flea market (Sundays) and the **Kulturbrauerei** (a converted brewery turned cultural center). Coffee and breakfast at a neighborhood café.

**Afternoon**: **KaDeWe** (Kaufhaus des Westens) — Europe's second-largest department store, with an extraordinary sixth-floor food hall. Not a shopping destination necessarily, but the food hall (fresh seafood, cheese, meats, pastries) is worth the visit. Then walk through **Kurfürstendamm** to the **Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church** (partially destroyed in WWII, preserved as a memorial).

**Evening**: Berlin's nightlife is world-famous. Options by preference:
- **Jazz**: A-Trane (Bleibtreustraße) — long-running jazz club, €10–15 entry
- **Classical**: Berliner Philharmonie (book far ahead at berliner-philharmoniker.de)
- **Club**: Berghain / Tresor / Watergate — Berlin's electronic music scene is genuinely world-class; note Berghain's selective door policy

---

## Day 7 — Berlin: Last Morning

**Morning options** (pick one based on interest):
- **Charlottenburg Palace**: Prussia's answer to Versailles — baroque palace with manicured gardens (30 minutes from center by U-Bahn, €12 entry)
- **Sachsenhausen Memorial**: Former Nazi concentration camp 35 km north (45 min by S-Bahn), free entry — somber but important
- **Hackescher Markt + Hackesche Höfe**: 8 interconnected Art Nouveau courtyards with independent shops, cafés, and galleries in Mitte
- **Markthalle Neun** (Thursdays and Sundays): Berlin's best street food market in Kreuzberg

**Afternoon**: Depart from Berlin Brandenburg Airport (BER) or continue onward.

---

## Getting Between Cities

**Munich → Berlin by train**: ICE from Munich Hauptbahnhof to Berlin Hauptbahnhof, 4 hours. Book at bahn.de; Sparpreis fares from €29 when booked 4+ weeks ahead. Up to €150 for same-day booking. Trains run hourly.

**By flight**: Munich–Berlin is a 1-hour flight, but accounting for airport time and transfer, the train is usually faster city-center to city-center.

---

## Extending the Itinerary

**Add Nuremberg** (between Munich and Berlin by train, 1 hour from Munich): The medieval walled city, the Nuremberg Trials Courthouse (now a museum), and the Christmas market (December, Germany's most famous).

**Add the Romantic Road**: Replace the Berlin portion with a drive or bus along the Romantic Road (Würzburg → Rothenburg ob der Tauber → Füssen) — Germany's most scenic tourist route through medieval towns.

**Add Hamburg**: 2 hours from Berlin by ICE. The Elbphilharmonie, the harbor, the Reeperbahn, and Germany's most port-influenced culture.
