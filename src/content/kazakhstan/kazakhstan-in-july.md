July is Kazakhstan's peak summer month. Almaty is warm (25–33°C) and the city's population heads to the mountain parks above the city to escape the heat. The Tian Shan high routes are at maximum accessibility — all passes are open, all huts operational. The steppe has browned from spring green to summer gold. And the traditional summer jailau (high pasture) culture of Kazakh nomadic life is at its most visible — yurts on the mountain meadows, horseback games, and the taste of the season's kumiss (fermented mare's milk).

## Weather & Conditions

**Almaty:** 22–33°C. Hot by afternoon. The mountains above are the escape.

**Tian Shan (high routes):** 10–20°C. Excellent. All passes open.

**Astana:** 20–30°C. Steppe summer. Hot but not extreme.

**Charyn Canyon:** 26–36°C. Very hot. Dawn visits only.

**Steppe:** 20–32°C. Brown and vast. Dramatic in a different way than spring.

## What to Do

**Jailau (high pasture) summer culture:** July is when Kazakh families traditionally move livestock to high mountain meadows. In the Tian Shan above Almaty, the Qarkara Valley, and the Jungar Alatau range, you can find working yurt camps where families live in the traditional felt tent for the summer. Cultural tour operators in Almaty arrange visits — some overnight, some day trips. Kumiss tasting, beshbarmak preparation, and horse riding with herders.

**Tian Shan Photography and Trekking:** July light on the Tian Shan peaks is extraordinary — long days, clear air, and the alpine meadows in full summer bloom. The circuit around Son-Kul Lake (in Kyrgyzstan, accessible on a combined Kazakhstan-Kyrgyzstan tour) gives a perspective on the broader nomadic plateau culture.

**Burabay National Park (near Astana):** The "Kazakhstan Switzerland" — a lake district with granite outcrops in the northern steppe, 220km from Astana. July is the most popular month for domestic tourism here. Kayaking, hiking, and the distinctive pine forest in the steppe landscape. The town of Schuchink is the base.

**Singing Dunes, Altyn-Emel:** In July's heat (arrive before 8am), the Singing Dunes produce their most consistent sound — an audible resonance when the sand vibrates. The 150m-high dune requires a short steep climb for the full effect.

**Caspian adventures, Mangystau region:** The lunar landscape of the Mangystau region (around Aktau) is best explored in July mornings. The Sherkala mountain, the Bozzhira canyon complex, and the Ustyurt plateau are extraordinary geological formations. All-day summer heat requires early starts and plenty of water.

## Festivals & Events

**Astana Day (July 6):** The capital's founding anniversary. Celebrations in Astana with cultural events.

**National Horse Games:** Various traditional Kazakh horse competitions (kokpar — goat-carcass polo, baige — long-distance horse racing, kyz kuu — kiss the girl horse chase) are held throughout the summer at regional events.

## Practical Tips

Charyn Canyon in July: arrive before 9am, leave before 11am. The canyon in midday July heat is genuinely brutal. Dawn start from Almaty (4 hours drive) for a morning visit and return before afternoon heat.

Burabay in July: the most popular domestic tourism destination in Kazakhstan during summer. Book accommodation 4–6 weeks ahead for July weekends.

Jailau visits: the best experiences are through operators who have genuine relationships with nomadic families rather than staged tourist versions. Almaty-based operators include Dostyk Tour and Kan Tengri.

## Who July Is For

Mountain trekkers and expedition climbers. Nomadic culture and jailau (high pasture) travelers. Anyone who specifically wants Kazakhstan's summer character — the heat, the gold steppe, and the mountain escape — as a genuine country experience.
