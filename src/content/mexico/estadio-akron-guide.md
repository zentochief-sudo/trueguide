Estadio Akron — officially the Estadio Chivas Akron — opened in 2010 and is one of the most modern football stadiums in Latin America. Designed by architect Rafael de la Peña, the stadium has a translucent membrane structure that illuminates the exterior at night and creates a distinctive visual atmosphere. It is owned by Club Deportivo Guadalajara (Chivas), the club with the largest following in Mexico.

---

## Stadium Facts

**Address**: Periférico Norte Manuel Gómez Morín 777, Zapopan, Jalisco  
**Capacity**: 49,850  
**Opened**: 2010  
**Home team**: Chivas de Guadalajara  
**World Cup 2026 matches**: 5 matches (group stage and round of 16)

---

## The Stadium and the Chivas

The Chivas are the only Liga MX team with a "Mexicans only" policy — since their founding, they have signed only Mexican nationals. This makes them the most representative club of the national football identity; their exclusive-use stadium (they share with no other team) is part of that identity.

Estadio Akron was built with state-of-the-art technology — natural grass, advanced sound systems, and ETFE membrane cladding (the same technology used in Beijing's Olympic Bird's Nest) place it among the most technically advanced stadiums in Latin America.

## Getting There

The stadium is in Zapopan, on the northern periphery of the Guadalajara metropolitan area — 20–30 minutes from the Historic Center in normal traffic.

### Tren Ligero and Macrobús

**Tren Ligero Line 1**: The north-south line reaches El Campanario station, approximately 3 km from the stadium. From El Campanario, Uber or a complementary bus completes the journey.

**Macrobús (BRT)**: The Macrobús corridor on Avenida Periférico Norte has stops near the stadium on event days. Check for special World Cup services announced by Guadalajara's transit system (SITEUR).

### Uber

The most direct option from the Historic Center or tourist neighborhoods:
- From Historic Center: 150–250 MXN, 25–40 minutes
- From Chapultepec/Americana: 120–200 MXN, 20–30 minutes
- Post-match: Price may rise 50–100 MXN due to demand

### Driving

Access via Periférico Norte. Official stadium parking: 100–150 MXN. On match days, the Periférico can experience significant congestion — arrive 2+ hours early.

## Match Day Experience

**Gates open**: 2 hours before kickoff.

**The exterior fan zone**: The stadium has a large exterior esplanade with food vendors, drinks, and merchandise on match days. Arriving 90 minutes early allows time to enjoy the pre-match atmosphere outside.

**Security**: Bag checks and prohibited items screening at all entrances. Containers larger than 500ml are not permitted.

**Weather**: The stadium roof protects most spectators from rain, which is likely in July (Guadalajara's rainy season). Early afternoon matches can be warm — light clothing and water are advisable.

---

## Inside the Stadium

**The translucent membrane**: The ETFE cladding allows sunlight in during the day and creates a lighting effect from inside at night — the stadium glows from outside like a giant lantern. For evening matches, the visual effect is extraordinary.

**The stands**: Four sectors. The north stand (Chivas) and south stand (visiting team) are the supporter sections. The lateral stands have the best viewing angles to the pitch.

**Food**: The stadium serves birria tacos, tortas, nachos, and beer. The birria tacos inside the stadium are not as good as in Tlaquepaque, but they're appropriate for the context.

**Acoustics**: The Akron's roof design concentrates sound effectively — with a full house, the crowd noise is notable.

---

## Around the Stadium

The immediate area around the stadium in Zapopan doesn't have the bar and restaurant density of more urban stadium locations. Pre-match options:
- Eat in Chapultepec or Tlaquepaque and go directly to the stadium
- The adjacent Andares shopping center has chain restaurant options
- The stadium esplanade has sufficient options for the pre-match period

---

## Practical Tips

- The stadium is in Zapopan municipality, outside central Guadalajara — plan travel time carefully.
- July means rain. Bring something waterproof even though the stadium is covered, as the journey to and from the stadium may be wet.
- For visitors attending multiple matches here, transport routes are the same each time but match-day traffic varies.
