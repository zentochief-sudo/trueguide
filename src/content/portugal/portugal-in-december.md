December is Portugal's Christmas month — and Portugal celebrates Christmas with genuine warmth and traditional culture. Lisbon and Porto illuminate their historic streets with Christmas lights. The Mercados de Natal (Christmas markets) appear in the city centers. Madeira runs the most spectacular New Year's Eve fireworks in Europe — the Funchal display is officially recognized as the world's largest fireworks show. And the continental coast is at its most dramatically Atlantic. December rewards specific intent: Christmas atmosphere, New Year in Madeira, or winter value in a mild European capital.

## Weather in December

**Lisbon:** 9°C to 15°C. Mild by European standards — a proper winter coat and umbrella, but rarely the cold of Paris or London. Rain is frequent but rarely persistent all day.

**Porto:** 7°C to 13°C. Cooler and wetter than Lisbon. Porto's December atmosphere — grey light, rain, the Port wine lodge illuminations reflecting on the Douro — is genuinely beautiful in its austerity.

**Algarve:** 12°C to 18°C. The warmest December in Portugal — mild enough for coastal walking every day. Not swimming weather but excellent for the Fishermen's Trail or beach walks.

**Alentejo:** 7°C to 13°C. Cold nights, comfortable days. The medieval towns are festive in a low-key, deeply local way.

**Madeira:** 16°C to 22°C. Year-round spring climate — December is genuinely warm by European standards. The island's famous eternal spring is at its most appreciated in winter.

## Madeira — New Year's Eve

Madeira's New Year's Eve celebration is one of the most spectacular in the world:

**The fireworks:** Funchal's New Year's Eve display holds the Guinness World Record for the world's largest fireworks show — the entire hillside amphitheater above the bay is covered with synchronized fireworks positions, producing a 360° display that lasts 8 minutes. The bay fills with cruise ships watching from the water.

**The buildup:** Funchal illuminates magnificently in December — the palm-lined seafront, the Mercado dos Lavradores, and the hills above the city. The city hosts a month of Christmas events before the New Year culmination.

**Accommodation for New Year:** Book Madeira New Year accommodation 6–8 months ahead — December 31 hotels in Funchal sell out entirely. The peninsula hotel strip (Lido area) and Reid's Palace (one of Europe's great historic hotels) command significant premium pricing for New Year's Eve.

**New Year's Day in Madeira:** January 1 brings the quietest day of the year — the city resting after the celebration. The levada walks on New Year's Day, with the island almost entirely to yourself, are excellent.

## Lisbon at Christmas

Lisbon's Christmas illuminations are genuinely attractive:

**Rua Augusta and Praça do Comércio:** The main downtown artery and riverside square — Christmas lights along the pedestrian street and over the praça. December evenings in the Baixa district are festive.

**Chiado and Bairro Alto:** The traditional shopping and cultural neighborhood — Christmas window displays at Livraria Bertrand (the world's oldest bookshop, 1732) and A Vida Portuguesa (the design shop dedicated to traditional Portuguese products).

**Mercado de Natal em Lisboa:** Christmas markets appear in several locations — Campo Grande park, the Praça do Comércio riverside, and the Parque Eduardo VII. Portuguese Christmas market culture is less developed than the German or French equivalent, but the Campo Grande market is pleasant.

**Presépio (Nativity scenes):** Traditional Portuguese Christmas culture centers on the presépio — elaborate Nativity scenes displayed in churches, public spaces, and homes throughout December. The Sé Cathedral and the Basílica da Estrela run notable displays.

**Christmas Eve (December 24):** The traditional Portuguese Christmas dinner — bacalhau (salted cod, prepared in one of its 365 traditional ways) on Christmas Eve. Portuguese families gather; restaurants are largely closed. Book Christmas Eve dining weeks ahead if traveling then.

## Porto in December

Porto in December is one of the most atmospheric winter city experiences in southern Europe:

**Christmas lights:** Porto's historic center — the Ribeira waterfront, the Rua de Santa Catarina pedestrian street, the Rua das Flores — is illuminated with Christmas lights. The reflections on the Douro of the Gaia wine lodge lights are distinctive.

**Port wine lodges:** December cave tastings at Taylor's, Sandeman, Graham's, and Ramos Pinto — small groups, attentive staff, and the caves themselves dramatically atmospheric in winter. The Taylor's cave is the most architecturally interesting.

**Livraria Lello:** The Art Nouveau bookshop on Rua das Carmelitas — December has manageable queues (nothing like July-August). The staircase, the painted ceiling, the glass skylight. Entry fee €5 (redeemable against purchases).

**Francesinha:** Porto's signature dish is particularly appropriate in December — the layers of cured meat, sausage, cheese, and spiced tomato-beer sauce is warming in a way that makes full sense in cold weather.

## The Algarve in December

The off-season Algarve in December:

**Coastal walking:** The Fishermen's Trail and the Vicentine coast are at their most dramatic — Atlantic winter seas, storm-cleaned beaches, the cliff paths empty. December walking on the Costa Vicentina is the best access to this landscape all year.

**Sagres and Cabo de São Vicente:** The southwestern tip of continental Europe — December storm waves against the cliffs are dramatic. The lighthouse and fort accessible and completely empty.

**Faro:** The Algarve capital — the old town island (Faro historic center is on an island in the Ria Formosa lagoon), the cathedral, the bone chapel at Nossa Senhora do Carmo church. December access is immediate.

Two-speed December: most of the month at near-November prices; Christmas week (Dec 22–26) and New Year (Dec 29–Jan 1) at seasonal spikes. Madeira New Year is a premium event — price accordingly.

## Practical Notes

- **December 8 (Imaculada Conceição):** National public holiday — many businesses close, tourist infrastructure operates normally.
- **December 25:** Christmas Day — most restaurants closed. Book ahead if traveling over Christmas; self-catering accommodation is the practical solution.
- **Madeira booking lead time:** New Year's Eve Madeira requires 6–8 month advance booking for hotels and flights. TAP Air Portugal, Ryanair, and EasyJet all serve Funchal from Lisbon (1.5 hours).
- **Christmas markets comparison:** Portuguese Christmas markets are smaller and less elaborate than Alsatian or German equivalents — Lisbon's December appeal is the Christmas illuminations and city atmosphere, not the markets specifically.

## The Short Version

December Portugal is Christmas warmth without European winter cold — Lisbon and Porto illuminated and festive, the Algarve warm enough for coastal walking, and Madeira delivering the most spectacular New Year's Eve fireworks on earth. The Madeira New Year requires early booking and carries premium pricing; everything else is near winter minimums. For winter travel in Europe, Portugal's December combines genuine mild weather, cultural atmosphere, and value that the northern capitals can't match.
