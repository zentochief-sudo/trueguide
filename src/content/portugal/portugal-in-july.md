## Algarve in July

The Algarve in July is peak beach season вЂ” plan accordingly:

**Eastern Algarve (Ria Formosa area):**
- **Ilha de Tavira:** Still the best beach вЂ” ferry access keeps car traffic out. July requires early arrival (8вЂ“9 AM) to secure space
- **Praia de Manta Rota:** The eastern quieter beaches вЂ” long Atlantic strand with lower density than the central Algarve

**Central Algarve:**
- **Praia da Marinha:** Iconic, crowded in July вЂ” go before 9 AM or after 5 PM for the crowds to thin
- **Benagil:** July kayak tours to the sea cave run every 30 minutes вЂ” book the earliest slot (8 or 8:30 AM)
- **Lagos:** The town's beaches (Dona Ana, Camilo) are accessible but crowded. The town itself remains lively without being unbearable

**Western Algarve / Costa Vicentina:**
- **Sagres:** The exposed Atlantic position keeps Sagres cooler and windier than the central coast вЂ” the beaches (Mareta, Martinhal) are less crowded in July
- **Praia de Odeceixe:** River-meets-sea beach вЂ” worth the drive up the Vicentine coast for a quieter July experience

**Practical:** Book Algarve accommodation 2вЂ“3 months ahead for July. Weekends at peak Algarve beaches require early arrival or late afternoon approach.

## Comporta and the Alentejo Coast

Comporta remains the less-crowded alternative to the Algarve вЂ” but July is when it gets discovered:

- **Praia de Comporta:** The pine-backed Atlantic beach вЂ” less crowded than Algarve equivalents but July weekends fill up
