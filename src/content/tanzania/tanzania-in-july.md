July is East Africa's peak safari month. The Great Migration has moved north into the Northern Serengeti and Masai Mara ecosystem. The Mara River crossings — wildebeest plunging into crocodile-filled water in their northward push — are beginning. The park roads are busy with safari vehicles. And every reputable camp in the Northern Serengeti is fully occupied. This is [Tanzania](/tanzania) at its most celebrated and its most expensive. Book everything six months ahead.

## Weather & Conditions

**Northern Serengeti:** 18–27°C. Dry, clear, cool nights. The grass is short. Wildlife visibility is excellent.

**[Ngorongoro](/tanzania/ngorongoro-guide):** 14–22°C. Perfect crater weather. Dry and clear.

**[Arusha](/tanzania/arusha-guide):** Dry season comfort. Cool evenings.

**[Zanzibar](/tanzania/zanzibar-guide):** Southeast monsoon continues. The east coast has wind and some cloud. North coast (Nungwi) is the best beach option.

**Mount [Kilimanjaro](/tanzania/kilimanjaro-guide):** Peak climbing season. July–August are the best months for summit attempts — dry, clear, and cold. Success rates on the upper mountain are highest.

## What to Do

**Mara River crossings, Northern Serengeti:** The Northern Serengeti between the Kogatende area (Tanzania) and the Kenya border at the Mara River is where July's defining drama plays out. Wildebeest gather on the south bank for hours or days, then suddenly surge into the crocodile-filled river. The crossings are violent, dramatic, and chaotic. The key camps: Asilia's Sayari, Migration Camp (Elewana), and Serengeti Bushtops have prime Mara River access. Expect to be at the river in a vehicle for hours at a time; bring patience, water, and a hat.

**Balloon safari, Central Serengeti:** Dawn hot air balloon flights over the Serengeti launch year-round but July's clear morning light makes them exceptional. The one-hour flight covers 20–30km of Serengeti landscape from 300m altitude. Book through camps or operators — Serengeti Balloon Safaris is the main operator. $600+ per person; worth it for the experience.

**Ngorongoro at peak:** The crater in July is at maximum visual impact — dry, clear, and full of game. The black rhino population (estimated 30–35 animals) is the main draw beyond the usual lion, elephant, and hippo. Crater access is permitted for day descents only (no overnight camping in the crater).

**Kilimanjaro summit push:** July is the best month statistically for Kilimanjaro summit success. The routes (Machame, Lemosho, Rongai) are fully operational. The summit night (typically starting at midnight from High Camp at 4,600m) is cold (-10 to -20°C at the summit) but the dawn view from Uhuru Peak (5,895m) is extraordinary.

**Pemba and Zanzibar diving:** July diving off Pemba Island is excellent. The Pemba Channel visibility exceeds 30m. Zanzibar's north coast has good diving conditions despite the southeast monsoon affecting the east coast.

## Festivals & Events

**Zanzibar International Film Festival (ZIFF, variable June or July):** Stonetown's most significant cultural event — East African, African, and international films in the Old Fort.

**Kilimanjaro Marathon (April — but July sees major charity events near the mountain):** Several international charity trekking events use July's reliable conditions for fundraising ascents.

## Practical Tips

July is Tanzania's most competitive booking month. Camps in the Northern Serengeti (Kogatende area) with Mara River access need to be booked 6+ months ahead. The best positioned camps — Sayari, Migration Camp, Serengeti Pioneer Camp — are fully booked by January for July dates.

Mara River crossings: the timing is unpredictable. Stay at river-access camps for a minimum of 3 nights to have reasonable crossing odds. A crossing can happen any day or not at all for a week.

Balloon safari bookings: 2–3 weeks ahead usually works for July, but specific launch sites may book out faster. Confirm through your camp.

## Who July Is For

First-time East Africa safari visitors who want the iconic Great Migration experience and are willing to pay peak prices. Kilimanjaro climbers. Wildlife photographers who have saved specifically for the Mara River crossing shot. July delivers; it also costs.
