Arrowhead Stadium opened in 1972 and has been the home of the Kansas City Chiefs NFL franchise since. It holds the Guinness World Record for loudest outdoor stadium — 142.2 decibels recorded during a 2014 Chiefs game — a product of its design: a bowl shape with steep upper decks that trap crowd noise over the field. For the 2026 World Cup, the configuration seats approximately **76,000**.

The stadium is part of the Truman Sports Complex on the east side of Kansas City — Arrowhead and the adjacent Kauffman Stadium (Kansas City Royals baseball) share a parking lot and the same highway interchange, 9 miles southeast of downtown.

---

## Getting There

**No direct public transit** connects downtown Kansas City to Arrowhead. The practical options:

**By Uber/Lyft**: From downtown (Power & Light District): 20–30 minutes, $25–40. This is the most practical option for visitors without a car. After the match, surge pricing: $40–70. Allow 30–45 minutes to get a ride post-match due to demand concentration.

**Official World Cup shuttles**: FIFA and local authorities typically organize shuttle services from designated downtown pick-up points during tournament matches. Check official FIFA 2026 transport information as match dates approach.

**By car**: I-70 east from downtown to the stadium exits. Parking on-site: $30–50 for standard lots. Arrive 90+ minutes before kickoff to avoid traffic. Post-match exit: 45–75 minutes to clear the area.

**By rideshare to the designated zone**: Uber drop-off is at the designated rideshare lot on the stadium's south side. Return pick-up is the same lot — walk past the car parking to reach it.

---

## Stadium Access and Entry

**Gates open**: 2.5 hours before kickoff for World Cup matches.

**Entry**: Electronic tickets via the FIFA ticketing app. No printed tickets accepted. Fully charge your phone before arriving — battery issues at the gate are the most common entry problem.

**Prohibited items**: No bags larger than 12"×6"×12" (clear bag policy). No outside food or drink. No professional cameras (detachable lens). Metal detectors and bag check at all gates — arrive early.

**The concourse**: Arrowhead has wide concourses with good sightlines from the stands — the design means you're never far from a food or beverage stand. The concourse level also has views of the field from most sections.

---

## Inside the Stadium

**The noise**: Arrowhead's bowl design with steep upper decks creates one of the loudest environments in sports. A full World Cup crowd — especially for a high-stakes match — will be loud. Ear protection is not necessary but the acoustics are notable.

**Sight lines**: All seats face the center of the field. The lower level is very close to the playing surface; the upper decks are steep but angled correctly. No bad seats in terms of orientation, though the upper corners are distant.

**Concessions**: American stadium food — hot dogs, nachos, BBQ (including burnt ends, in some concession areas), and beer. Prices are stadium-inflated: $12–18 for food items, $12–16 for beer. Credit cards accepted at all stands.

---

## Before and After the Match

**Pre-match tailgate culture**: Kansas City Chiefs games are known for tailgate parties in the parking lots — grills, beer, and the specific ritual of American football tailgating. The World Cup will attract a different crowd but the parking lot pre-match atmosphere is likely to be active regardless.

**Eating near the stadium**: The stadium complex has limited restaurant options beyond the stadium itself. Pre-match eating is better done downtown or in the Crossroads District, then Ubering to the game. Post-match eating: most downtown restaurants are closer than any stadium-area options.
