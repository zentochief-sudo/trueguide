Boston has the oldest subway system in the United States (opened 1897) and one of the most walkable downtown cores of any World Cup host city. The T (MBTA) handles most visitor needs; the Logan Airport Blue Line connection ($2.90) makes the airport transit one of the most straightforward in the country. The one complexity is the 28-mile commute to [Gillette Stadium](/united-states/gillette-stadium-guide), which requires the commuter rail.

---

## The T (MBTA Subway)

The MBTA subway has four color-coded lines. For World Cup visitors, the relevant lines:

**Red Line**: Cambridge (Harvard, MIT, Kendall Square) to downtown (Park Street, Downtown Crossing) to South Station. Airport connection via Silver Line.

**Orange Line**: Back Bay and Ruggles to Downtown Crossing to North Station. Covers the South End neighborhood.

**Green Line**: A surface light rail extending from Lechmere in Cambridge through downtown (Park Street, Boylston, Copley) and west into Brookline. Serves Back Bay (Copley Station) and the Fenway area.

**Blue Line**: East Boston (Logan Airport) to downtown (Aquarium, Government Center, Bowdoin). The airport connection.

**Key stations for visitors**:
- **South Station**: Commuter rail hub (to Gillette Stadium), Red Line, Silver Line (to airport). Central transit hub.
- **Park Street**: Downtown junction of Red and Green Lines. Most Freedom Trail hotels are within walking distance.
- **Copley**: Back Bay, Newbury Street, the Boston Marathon finish line area.
- **Airport**: Blue Line station at Logan's terminal area.

**Fare**: $2.90/ride with CharlieCard (the transit card). Cash fare: $3.25 (paper ticket). CharlieCard available at airport and downtown station machines.

**Day pass**: $13 unlimited rides. Worth buying for match days with multiple T connections.

## Getting to Gillette Stadium

**MBTA Commuter Rail (Providence/Stoughton Line)**

Event-day trains to Foxborough Station are the correct transit option for stadium access.

- **Depart South Station**: Trains run 2–3 hours before kickoff on event days.
- **Journey time**: 70–80 minutes from South Station.
- **Cost**: Zone 5 fare; approximately $12–16 round-trip.
- **Book in advance**: Event trains sell out. Purchase at mbta.com or the MBTA commuter rail ticket machines at South Station.
- **Return**: Trains depart Foxborough after the final whistle. Limited capacity — be at the platform within 15 minutes of the match ending.

**From Providence, RI**: 20–30 minutes from Providence Station to Foxborough. Visitors based in Providence can use this as an alternative commute chain.

**Driving**: I-93 south from Boston, I-95 south, exit 9. 30–45 minutes without traffic; 60–90 minutes on match days. Parking: $40–60/vehicle. The commuter rail is faster and less stressful.

## Logan International Airport

Logan Airport is 3 miles east of downtown Boston across the harbor. The transit options are unusually good for a major US airport.

**Blue Line subway**: Airport Station (accessible via free Massport shuttle between terminals and the subway station) to downtown Boston. Journey time: 12–18 minutes to Government Center/Park Street. Cost: $2.90. The best value airport transit of any East Coast World Cup host city.

**Silver Line (SL1 bus)**: Free from all airport terminals to South Station — a direct connection to the commuter rail hub. Journey time: 20–30 minutes depending on tunnel traffic.

**Water Taxi**: Rowes Wharf Water Taxi runs between Logan's ferry dock and the Rowes Wharf hotel/restaurant complex downtown. 7 minutes, $20. A genuine experience of Boston Harbor.

**Rideshare**: $25–45 to downtown. The tunnel approach means variable traffic; Blue Line is faster and cheaper in almost all scenarios.

## Walking

Boston is the most walkable World Cup host city. Key walk times from central downtown:

- Downtown to North End (Paul Revere House): 15 minutes
- Downtown to Beacon Hill: 12 minutes  
- Downtown to Back Bay (Copley Square): 20 minutes
- Downtown to South End (Toro restaurant): 25 minutes
- Boston to Cambridge: Not walkable (30-minute Red Line ride, 40-minute walk via Cambridge Bridge)

The Freedom Trail self-guided route is entirely walkable and connects the major historical sites in a 2.5-mile path.

## Ferries

The MBTA Commuter Boat runs between Long Wharf (downtown waterfront) and Charlestown, Hingham, and Hull. Not primary transit for World Cup visitors, but the Charlestown ferry ($3.70) is the scenic approach to the USS Constitution (Old Ironsides) in the Charlestown Navy Yard — a 10-minute crossing that is an alternative to walking or taking the T.

## Practical Notes

**CharlieCard**: Get one at Logan Airport upon arrival — the convenience of tap-to-ride on all T lines and buses is worth the machine transaction. No card fee; the card is free with an initial load.

**Traffic**: Boston's traffic is among the worst in the US for a mid-size city — the legacy street grid (pre-planned, irregular) combined with high population density creates gridlock. Do not attempt to drive within Boston itself; rideshare and walking are better for neighborhood-to-neighborhood trips.

**The Big Dig legacy**: Boston buried I-93 underground through the city center (the Big Dig, completed 2003) and replaced the elevated freeway with the Rose Kennedy Greenway — a park. The Greenway is walkable from downtown hotels to the North End.
