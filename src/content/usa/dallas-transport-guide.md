Dallas is a car city. The freeway system — the Mixmaster interchange, the Dallas North Tollway, the network radiating outward from downtown — was built for a city that assumed everyone would drive everywhere. The DART light rail and bus system has expanded significantly since the 1990s and now covers major corridors, but it cannot substitute for a car if you want to explore broadly.

For World Cup visitors concentrated in the Uptown–Downtown–Deep Ellum triangle with match-day trips to Arlington, transit is workable. For visitors who want the full DFW Metroplex experience, a rental car is the honest recommendation.

---

## DART Light Rail

Dallas Area Rapid Transit (DART) operates four light rail lines covering 93 miles. The system is clean, air-conditioned, and runs frequently during peak hours.

**Key lines for World Cup visitors**:

**Red and Blue Lines**: Run north-south through downtown Dallas, from Plano and Richardson in the north to Westmoreland and Ledbetter in the south. These lines cover the downtown core with multiple stations including **Pearl/Arts District**, **Akard**, **St. Paul**, and **Convention Center**.

**Orange Line**: Connects downtown Dallas to DFW Airport (Terminal A). Journey from DFW to downtown: approximately 45–55 minutes. This is the primary airport transit option.

**Green Line**: East-west line connecting downtown to Deep Ellum (Deep Ellum station), East Dallas, and Fair Park. The **Deep Ellum station** makes this useful for evening entertainment trips.

**How to pay**: The **GoPass app** (iOS/Android) allows mobile ticket purchase for all DART services and the Trinity Railway Express. A single DART ride is $2.50. An all-day pass is $6 and covers unlimited DART rail and bus plus TRE (the commuter rail to Arlington). Download and set up before your trip.

TAP cards are also available at DART station machines ($0 card, $2.50/ride or $6/day pass).

## Trinity Railway Express (TRE) — For AT&T Stadium

The TRE commuter rail connects **Dallas Union Station** with **Fort Worth T&P Station**, with stops including **CentrePort/DFW Airport** — the station closest to AT&T Stadium for World Cup match days.

**Match-day shuttle service**: Buses will operate between CentrePort station and AT&T Stadium on match days. Shuttle journey: 10–15 minutes. Service typically begins 2.5 hours before kickoff and continues 1.5 hours after the final whistle.

**Full route from downtown Dallas to AT&T Stadium**:
1. Take any DART rail line to **Dallas Union Station** (downtown)
2. Board TRE westbound to **CentrePort/DFW Airport** (~30 minutes)
3. Board match-day shuttle to stadium (~15 minutes)
4. Total: 55–75 minutes from downtown

**Cost**: A GoPass all-day pass ($6) covers both DART and TRE. This is significantly cheaper than match-day rideshare ($65–100 surge).

**Return journey**: The post-match return from CentrePort to Dallas Union Station runs the same route. TRE trains run frequently post-match. From Union Station, DART rail connects to your hotel.

## Airports

### Dallas/Fort Worth International (DFW)

**DART Orange Line**: Free shuttle from any DFW terminal to Terminal A, then Orange Line directly to downtown Dallas. Journey to Pearl/Arts District: 45–55 minutes. $2.50 fare. The recommended transit option.

**Rideshare**: $35–55 to Uptown/Downtown, 25–40 minutes depending on traffic. The DFW rideshare pickup area is at Terminal E (Level 1). All terminals have a free Terminal Link train to reach Terminal E.

**Taxi**: Available at all terminals, metered. Approximately $45–60 to downtown Dallas.

### Dallas Love Field (DAL)

No direct rail connection. Options:
- **Rideshare**: $15–25 to Uptown/Downtown, 15–20 minutes. The most practical option.
- **Bus**: DART bus route 524 connects Love Field to the Inwood/Love Field station on the Orange Line. Infrequent service; only practical for budget travelers with time flexibility.

## Rental Cars

A rental car opens up everything that DART doesn't cover: Fort Worth, Plano, McKinney, Arlington on non-match days, day trips to Waco or the Hill Country.

**From DFW Airport**: The consolidated rental center is accessible via the Airport Terminal Link train from any terminal. All major companies. Weekly rates: $250–400 for an economy car.

**Driving in Dallas**:
- The freeway system is extensive and generally fast outside rush hour
- Rush hour: 7–9am and 4–7pm on I-35E, I-30, and the Dallas North Tollway
- The speed limits on Texas highways (75 mph on some stretches) mean that out-of-city trips are faster than they appear on maps
- Parking: Uptown and downtown have paid garages at $10–20/day. Free street parking is limited in commercial areas but exists in residential neighborhoods.

**Gas**: Texas gas prices are typically among the lowest in the country (below the US average). Fill up at stations on US-75 or I-35 rather than at airport-adjacent stations.

## Rideshare

Uber and Lyft both operate extensively in Dallas. Standard pricing is lower than NYC or LA.

**Typical fares**:
- Uptown to Deep Ellum: $10–15
- Downtown to Bishop Arts: $12–18
- Uptown to AT&T Stadium (Arlington): $35–55 normal, $65–100 match-day surge
- DFW Airport to Uptown: $35–55

**Match-day surge management**: Pre-schedule your ride using Uber's scheduled ride feature. Alternatively, walk 5–10 minutes from the stadium perimeter before requesting post-match — this helps with both surge and pickup times.

## Bike Share

**Dallas B-Cycle**: A docked bike share system with stations primarily in Uptown, the Arts District, and downtown. $3.50 per 30-minute ride or a $12/day unlimited pass. Practical for short trips within the Uptown–downtown corridor where the bike infrastructure is reasonable.

Dallas's cycling infrastructure is limited compared to cities like Seattle or San Francisco. Stick to the dedicated trail along **Katy Trail** (a paved rail-trail running 3.5 miles through Uptown and East Dallas) for safe, pleasant cycling.

---

## The McKinney Avenue Trolley

The vintage M-Line Trolley runs for free along McKinney Avenue from the St. Paul DART station (downtown) north through the heart of Uptown. Runs daily, approximately every 15–20 minutes. Slow (you can walk faster) but a pleasant way to traverse McKinney Avenue without driving. Useful for moving between restaurants along the strip without summoning a rideshare.

## Practical Tips

**GoPass app**: Download before your trip. Set up payment. The interface shows real-time DART and TRE departure times as well as ticket purchase. This is the single most useful transit tool for Dallas.

**Traffic apps**: Waze is more useful than Google Maps for Dallas freeway routing — the city's real-time traffic data integration is better. Keep it open for any freeway driving.

**Avoiding rush hour**: If you have a rental car, the window 9:30am–3:30pm and after 8pm is when freeway driving is genuinely pleasant. The 4–7pm window on I-35E and I-30 is Dallas at its worst.
