Gillette Stadium opened in 2002 as the home of the New England Patriots NFL franchise and the New England Revolution MLS club. Located in Foxborough, Massachusetts — a town of 18,000 people that exists in an otherwise rural/suburban landscape 28 miles south of Boston — it presents the unusual World Cup scenario of a stadium in a very small town, requiring most of the city's visitors to make a long commute each match day.

---

## Stadium Facts

**Address**: One Patriot Place, Foxborough, MA 02035  
**Capacity**: 65,878  
**Surface**: FieldTurf artificial (likely replaced with natural grass for World Cup)  
**Roof**: Open-air; no rain cover  
**World Cup matches**: 6 matches  
**Home teams**: New England Patriots (NFL), New England Revolution (MLS)

---

## Getting There

### MBTA Commuter Rail (Recommended)

The **Providence/Stoughton Line** of the MBTA runs special trains to **Foxborough Station** on major event days. This is the correct transit option for visitors based in Boston.

**Departure points**: 
- **South Station** (the primary Boston hub): 70–80 minutes to Foxborough
- **Back Bay Station**: 75–85 minutes

**Trains**: Event-day service only — trains depart South Station several hours before kickoff and return after the final whistle. Not regular-schedule; book tickets in advance at mbta.com or via the MBTA app.

**Cost**: Approximately $12–16 round-trip. Zone 5 fare.

**From the Foxborough Station to the stadium**: The station is approximately 0.5 miles from the stadium main entrance. There is a walkway; MBTA also operates a shuttle on event days. Allow 10–15 minutes from the train to the stadium gate.

### From Providence, Rhode Island

Providence Amtrak/commuter rail station is only 20 miles from Foxborough. The MBTA commuter rail operates in both directions on event days. For visitors staying in Providence, the match-day commute is faster than from Boston (approximately 30 minutes).

### Driving

I-93 south to I-95 south (from Boston). Exit 9 to Route 1 south to Gillette Stadium. 

**Parking**: Multiple lots around the stadium. Pre-purchase online: $40–60/vehicle. The Patriot Place shopping center adjacent to the stadium has a large parking complex.

**Traffic**: Post-match congestion on Route 1 and the I-95 on-ramps is significant — 45–75 minutes to clear the area. Commuter rail eliminates this entirely.

## The Stadium Environment

**Foxborough context**: Foxborough is a small New England town with little pre-match infrastructure beyond the Patriot Place development (a shopping center and restaurant complex adjacent to the stadium). The pre-match experience is concentrated on:

**Patriot Place**: The attached shopping, restaurant, and entertainment complex — Bass Pro Shops, a cinema, and a collection of chain restaurants. Not the most interesting pre-match environment, but functional. Wrentham Village Premium Outlets is 5 miles south for shopping.

**The stadium plaza**: FIFA typically activates the area around the stadium gate with food vendors, entertainment, and fan activities. The wide concourse approach to Gillette is suitable for this use.

## Inside the Stadium

**Open-air design**: Gillette Stadium has no roof — July weather in Massachusetts is typically warm (24–28°C) with possible afternoon thunderstorms. Afternoon matches may have sun or rain depending on conditions; evening matches are comfortable.

**Views**: The stadium has good sightlines throughout. The corner sections have the longest lines to the pitch but remain acceptable. Premium Club sections are in the western grandstand (more shade).

**Concessions**: New England sports stadiums have improved their food programs. Expect lobster rolls (seasonal but symbolic), clam chowder, and the standard sports venue offerings.

**Beer**: $12–15/pint. Draft selections include local New England craft options alongside macrobrews.

## Match Day Logistics Summary

Given the 28-mile distance from Boston to Foxborough, logistics require more planning than at any other US World Cup venue:

**Recommended timeline (70-minute commuter rail journey)**:
- South Station departure: 2 hours before kickoff
- Arrive Foxborough Station: 80 minutes before kickoff
- Walk/shuttle to stadium: 15 minutes
- Security and entry: 30–40 minutes before kickoff

**Return**: Commuter trains depart Foxborough shortly after the final whistle. Trains fill quickly; be at the station within 20 minutes of the end of the match.

**One alternative**: Stay in Providence and commute from there. The Providence-to-Foxborough journey is 30 minutes instead of 80; Providence has a complete hotel inventory and a food scene worth discovering independently.

---

## Practical Tips

- Book commuter rail event trains immediately when FIFA announces the match schedule. They sell out.
- Driving is rational only if staying in South of Boston suburbs or if three or more people share a car (parking $40–60 split among passengers).
- The Foxborough commute is the longest of any US World Cup host city. Build the extra transit time into every match day plan.
- Weather contingency: Open-air stadium in New England in July means rain is plausible. Pack a light rain layer that fits under a jersey.
