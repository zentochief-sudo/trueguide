Houston is among the most affordable World Cup cities in the United States, with a combination of lower hotel rates than the coastal cities, some of the best cheap food in the country (Vietnamese, Nigerian, Tex-Mex all available for $12–16 per meal), and a direct METRORail connection to NRG Stadium that costs $1.25 each way.

The main cost variable is airport transport — George Bush Intercontinental (IAH) has no rail connection, meaning a $40–60 rideshare from the airport. Choosing Hobby Airport (HOU) eliminates this with a $1.25 rail connection.

---

## Accommodation

**Budget hotels**: $90–140/night for national chains along the METRORail corridor (Midtown, Medical Center). The best budget value in the city.

**Mid-range hotels**: $150–240/night in Midtown, Museum District, or downtown. The **Crowne Plaza Houston Midtown** and similar properties offer good rates relative to amenity level.

**Airbnb**: Excellent value, particularly in Montrose and The Heights. A private room: $65–95/night. A one-bedroom apartment: $85–140/night.

**Upscale**: $220–380/night at the Marriott Marquis, The Post Houston, or Hôtel Alessandra.

**World Cup premium**: 30–50% above base rates during match weeks. Book 3–4 months ahead.

---

## Transport

**METRORail single ride**: $1.25. A round trip to NRG Stadium costs $2.50 — the best match-day transit value of any US World Cup city.

**Day pass**: $3 (unlimited rides). Worth buying if making 3+ trips in a day.

**Airport rideshare**:
- IAH to downtown: $40–60 (no rail option)
- HOU to downtown: $20–30 (or $1.25 by Purple Line)

**Rental car**: $40–65/day plus parking ($10–20/day in most areas).

**Rideshare within the city**: $10–22 for standard cross-neighborhood trips.

## Food

Houston offers the lowest food cost-to-quality ratio of any World Cup city.

**$25–35/day eating well**:
- Breakfast: Vietnamese iced coffee + bánh mì at Lee's Sandwiches ($6–8)
- Lunch: Pho at a Bellaire Boulevard restaurant ($12–14)
- Dinner: Nigerian jollof plate at The Jollof Kitchen ($12–16)

**$65–85/day at real restaurants**:
- One restaurant dinner ($35–45) plus budget breakfasts and lunches

**The specific value case**: Houston's Vietnamese food is priced at Vietnamese community prices, not tourist prices. A large pho bowl at the real Bellaire Boulevard operations is $12–14. The same bowl in a trendy restaurant in New York is $18–22.

## Attractions

**Free**:
- Discovery Green Park (downtown)
- Buffalo Bayou Park (hike/bike trail)
- Houston Museum of Fine Arts (free Thursdays)
- Hermann Park

**Worth paying for**:
- Houston Museum of Natural Science: $25–30
- Space Center Houston: $33 (book tram tours in advance)
- Houston Zoo: $23

## Sample Daily Budgets

**Budget traveler ($80–100/day)**:
- Budget hotel (Medical Center): $95
- Food: $30 (Vietnamese pho, bánh mì, street tacos)
- Transport: $3 (METRORail day pass)
- Attraction: $0 (Hermann Park, Buffalo Bayou)
- Tips: $6
- **Total: ~$134** — the hotel is the driver; Airbnb drops this to ~$100

**Mid-range ($200–240/day)**:
- Mid-range hotel (Midtown): $180
- Food: $65 (one restaurant + casual others)
- Transport: $15 (rail + one rideshare)
- Attraction: $25 (Space Center or HMNS)
- Tips: $13
- **Total: ~$298**

**Match-day transport**: $2.50 round trip by METRORail. The best match-day transit deal of any World Cup city.
