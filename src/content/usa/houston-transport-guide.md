Houston is a car city — larger in geographic area than the entire state of Rhode Island, built around freeways, with most of its neighborhoods only practical by car. But for World Cup visitors, the METRORail Red Line provides the one genuinely excellent transit connection that matters most: a direct rail ride from downtown to NRG Stadium in 15–20 minutes for $1.25.

For exploring Houston beyond the downtown-to-stadium corridor, a rental car or rideshare is the realistic option. Understanding both systems will make the week work.

---

## METRORail

Houston METRO operates three light rail lines. For World Cup visitors, only the Red Line matters consistently.

**Red Line (North-South)**: Runs from Northline Transit Center in the north through downtown (multiple Main Street stations), through Midtown, the Museum District, the Texas Medical Center, and to the Fannin South Transit Center. Critically, it has a stop at **NRG Park/Astrodome** — a 3-minute walk from NRG Stadium entrance.

Key Red Line stations:
- **Main Street Square** (downtown): 15–20 min to NRG Stadium
- **Bell** / **McGowen** (Midtown): 12–15 min to NRG Stadium  
- **Museum District**: 10 min to NRG Stadium
- **NRG Park / Astrodome**: Stadium stop

**Green and Purple Lines**: Serve East Houston and the University of Houston area (Green), and Hobby Airport to downtown (Purple). Useful for airport access from Hobby and exploring areas east of downtown, but not relevant for stadium transit.

**How to pay**: Q card (available at station machines, $1.50 card fee, $1.25 per ride) or cash. Contactless credit/debit card payment accepted at newer validators. A day pass is $3 (3 rides at full price — only worth it for 3+ trips). Most visitors buying individual rides will spend $2.50 round trip to the stadium.

**Service frequency**: Every 6–12 minutes during peak hours. Match-day service increases to every 4–6 minutes.

## Getting to NRG Stadium on Match Days

The recommended transit route:

1. Walk or rideshare to any Red Line station
2. Board Red Line southbound toward Fannin South
3. Ride to **NRG Park/Astrodome** station
4. Walk 3–5 minutes to stadium entrance
5. Total cost: $1.25 each way

This is the most transit-efficient World Cup stadium connection in Texas.

**Return**: Board Red Line northbound at NRG Park station. Trains run frequently post-match. Expect a 10–20 minute wait at the platform during the immediate post-match period.

## Airports

### George Bush Intercontinental Airport (IAH)

Houston's main international airport, 25 miles north of downtown. There is no rail connection — a significant infrastructure gap.

**Rideshare**: $40–60 to downtown, 30–45 minutes in normal traffic. The practical choice for most visitors.

**METRO Bus Route 102**: Connects IAH (Terminal C) to downtown Houston (Main Street/Polk Transit Center). Journey time: 65–80 minutes. Cost: $3.50. Infrequent service — check schedules before relying on it.

**Taxi**: Available outside all IAH terminals. Flat rate to downtown: approximately $60.

### William P. Hobby Airport (HOU)

Hobby is 9 miles southeast of downtown — the closer, more transit-friendly airport, primarily served by Southwest Airlines.

**METRORail Purple Line**: From Hobby Airport station to downtown (Fannin South connection to Red Line). Total journey to downtown: 45–55 minutes. $1.25. The best airport transit option in Houston.

**Rideshare**: $20–30 to downtown, 20–30 minutes.

**For World Cup visitors**: If your flight options include both IAH and HOU at comparable prices, HOU offers significantly better transit access and is closer to the city. The Purple Line to downtown, then Red Line to NRG, makes Hobby the most practically useful airport for the match-day transit chain.

## Rental Cars

A rental car expands access beyond the Red Line corridor substantially — The Heights, Southwest Houston's Vietnamese food district, Space Center Houston, and Galveston beach day trips all require a car.

**From IAH**: Rental car center accessible via a free shuttle from all terminals. Weekly rates: $250–420. All major companies.

**From HOU**: Rental counters on-site. Similar rates.

**Driving in Houston**:
- **The Loop** (I-610): The inner loop freeway that rings the urban core. Most tourist-relevant neighborhoods are inside or adjacent to the Loop.
- **Rush hour**: 7–9am and 4–7pm. The 610/I-10 interchange (Spaghetti Bowl) and the 59/610 interchange are the worst congestion points.
- **The Medical Center corridors**: Holcombe Boulevard and Main Street south of downtown get very heavy during shift changes at the medical complex.
- **Speed**: Houston freeways technically run at 65 mph; 70–75 is the actual flow speed. The city's geography means freeway driving covers substantial distances quickly when traffic cooperates.

**Parking**: Midtown and downtown garages: $10–20/day. Most Uptown hotels include parking. Free street parking exists in residential areas surrounding the commercial strips.

## Rideshare

Uber and Lyft are both extensively available. Pricing is competitive and generally lower than NYC or LA.

**Typical fares** (non-match day):
- Downtown to Montrose: $10–15
- Downtown to Bellaire (Vietnamese food corridor): $18–25
- Museum District to downtown: $12–15
- Midtown to The Heights: $14–20

**IAH to downtown**: $40–60 (significantly cheaper by rideshare than taxi).

**Match-day surge**: From downtown to NRG, surge pricing during the 90 minutes before kickoff: $35–60. The METRORail is dramatically better value. Post-match, rideshare from NRG: $25–50 depending on surge.

## Bike Share

**BCycle Houston**: A docked bike share system with stations throughout Midtown, Montrose, downtown, and the Museum District. $3.50 per 30-minute ride, $12/day unlimited. The bike infrastructure in Midtown and along the Buffalo Bayou corridor is reasonable for cycling.

**Buffalo Bayou Park**: A 10-mile hike and bike trail along the bayou through downtown — the best free outdoor activity accessible from the central neighborhoods. The trail connects to Eleanor Tinsley Park on the west and to the bayou promenade east of downtown.

## Practical Apps

**METRO Trip** (official METRO app): Real-time arrival predictions for all METRO rail and bus.

**Google Maps**: Works well for Houston transit routing. Citymapper is less optimized for Houston than for NYC.

**Waze**: Better than Google Maps for Houston freeway routing — particularly useful for navigating around the Loop and the complex spaghetti interchanges.
