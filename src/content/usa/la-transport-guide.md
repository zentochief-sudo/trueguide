Los Angeles has a reputation for being impossible to navigate without a car. This reputation is about 60% accurate and 40% outdated. The Metro system has expanded significantly since 2010, and for a visitor whose movements cluster around a handful of key corridors, it is genuinely usable. The car remains necessary for exploring broadly, but it is not the only option.

What follows is the honest account of every transport option, when it works, and when it does not.

---

## The TAP Card

Before anything else: get a TAP card. This is the contactless transit card used on all LA Metro services — subway, light rail, buses, and Metro Micro.

Pick one up at any Metro station from the vending machines ($2 card fee). Load $20–30 onto it at the machine or via the TAP app. Each Metro ride costs $1.75. Day passes ($7) are worth it if you're making four or more trips. Weekly passes ($25) are excellent value for a full week of transit use.

The card works on all Metro rail lines, all Metro bus routes, and the Metrolink commuter rail. Keep it accessible; you tap in at every rail station and on entry to every bus.

## The Metro Rail Network

LA Metro operates six rail lines that, together, cover the most useful tourist corridors reasonably well.

**B Line (Red Line)** — The backbone. Runs from North [Hollywood](/united-states/hollywood-guide) through Hollywood (Highland, Vine, Vermont/Sunset, Vermont/Beverly) to downtown Union Station via MacArthur Park and Westlake. If you're staying in Hollywood, this line is your primary connection to downtown. ~20 minutes Hollywood to downtown.

**D Line (Purple Line)** — Shares the downtown stations with the B Line, then extends west through Koreatown (Wilshire/Vermont, Wilshire/Normandie, Wilshire/Western) toward the Westside. The extension to Westwood/UCLA opens in 2026, which means for the World Cup, you will for the first time be able to take Metro from Hollywood to UCLA in a single seat. This is a significant addition.

**E Line (Expo Line)** — Runs from downtown Santa Monica through Culver City to downtown. Key stations: Santa Monica, 26th Street, Bergamot, Culver City, Jefferson/USC, then downtown. This is the primary Westside connection. ~45 minutes Santa Monica to downtown.

**A Line (Gold Line)** — Downtown to Pasadena and beyond, plus a southern branch toward Long Beach. Useful for day trips to Pasadena (30 minutes from Union Station).

**C Line (Green Line)** — Runs east-west through the South Bay, connecting to the K Line at Redondo Beach. Useful if you're coming from the airport corridor.

**K Line (Crenshaw/LAX Line)** — The line you need for [SoFi Stadium](/united-states/sofi-stadium-guide). Runs from the E Line's Expo/Crenshaw station south through Inglewood to Westchester/Veterans, with a stop at Inglewood – Fairview Heights approximately 1.5 miles from the stadium. Match-day shuttles connect the station to the stadium entrance. Full journey from downtown: 45–55 minutes. From Santa Monica (via E Line transfer at Expo/Crenshaw): 55–65 minutes.

## Getting to SoFi Stadium

**The recommended route** for most visitors:

1. From your hotel, get to any Metro rail station
2. Travel to the **Expo/Crenshaw** station (accessible from the E Line east or the C/K Line south)
3. Transfer to the **K Line** southbound
4. Ride to **Inglewood – Fairview Heights**
5. Board the World Cup shuttle to the stadium (free with match ticket, running from 2 hours before kickoff)

Total journey from Santa Monica: 55–65 minutes
Total journey from Hollywood: 65–75 minutes (B Line to downtown, transfer to E Line or A Line, then K Line)
Total journey from Koreatown: 45–55 minutes (D Line to Wilshire/Vermont, E or K Line)

**Rideshare alternative**: Budget $60–120 each way from the Westside on match days. Prices surge significantly in the 2–3 hours before and after matches. If you're using rideshare, schedule your pickup from outside the match-day surge zone if possible — walking 10 minutes from the stadium entrance before requesting reduces the price by 30–50%.

**Driving**: Not recommended on match days. Parking at SoFi starts at $50–80 (pre-purchased; significantly more on the day). The 405/105 interchange near the stadium processes tens of thousands of vehicles after large events. Expect 60–90 minutes to travel 10 miles post-match.

## The Car Rental Option

If your plan extends beyond the Westside–downtown corridor, a rental car will significantly expand what's accessible. The practical calculus:

**Rent a car if**: You want to visit Malibu, the San Gabriel Valley, Pasadena, Griffith Park (driving), the Valley, or anywhere not on a Metro line. If you have more than 4–5 days in LA and plan to explore beyond the main tourist corridors.

**Don't rent a car if**: You're staying in Santa Monica or Hollywood for 2–3 days focused on matches and nearby food. The Metro covers your needs and parking costs will add $20–30/day.

**Where to rent**: LAX is the biggest rental hub — a free shuttle from the arrivals terminal takes you to the consolidated rental facility. Weekly rates: $250–450 for an economy car in summer. Book 4–6 weeks ahead for better rates.

**Downtown and Hollywood** also have rental locations (Enterprise, Hertz, Budget) accessible without going to the airport.

**The driving rules**:
- Rush hour is 7–10am and 4–8pm. These windows are not suggestions. A 15-mile freeway trip that takes 25 minutes at 11am takes 75 minutes at 5:30pm.
- The 405 is the worst freeway in the city. The 10 east of downtown and the 101 near the Hollywood interchange are close behind.
- Google Maps and Waze are both excellent for LA navigation. Check them before leaving, not during.
- Parking: expect $15–25/day at hotel parking, $5–12/hour at commercial garages near attractions.

## Rideshare (Uber / Lyft)

Rideshare is practical for trips that are too short to justify a rental car but too inconvenient for Metro. The key variables:

**Normal pricing**: $15–25 for most cross-city trips (Hollywood to Silver Lake, Santa Monica to Culver City)

**Match-day pricing**: $60–120 from the Westside or Hollywood to SoFi Stadium. Plan for surge. The price tends to be highest 90 minutes before kickoff and immediately after the final whistle. If you can take Metro to within 2–3 miles of the stadium and rideshare the last leg from a residential street, you'll pay significantly less.

**Late-night**: After midnight, rideshare is the most practical option for most neighborhoods. Queue times are longer on weekends (3–8 minutes). Keep the app open before you need it.

## LAX and Getting to the City

**From LAX to your hotel**:

The **Metro K Line** now connects directly to LAX — take the free Automated People Mover from the arrivals terminal to the Aviation/96th St Metro station, then board the K Line north. Travel time to downtown: approximately 45–55 minutes. This is the cheapest option ($1.75 with TAP card) and avoids the notorious LAX traffic entirely.

**Rideshare from LAX**: Budget $35–60 to Hollywood or Santa Monica under normal conditions. Surge pricing during peak travel periods can push this to $80–120. The rideshare pickup area at LAX is a dedicated facility — follow the signs to "Rideshares/TNCs" after collecting luggage.

**Hotel shuttles**: Some hotels in the airport corridor offer free shuttles. Call ahead to confirm.

**Taxis**: Available at the taxi stand outside each terminal. Fixed rates to specific zones: approximately $65 to Hollywood, $45 to Santa Monica.

## Cycling

Los Angeles has invested heavily in bike infrastructure since 2015, and some corridors are now genuinely bikeable.

**The Marvin Braude Bike Trail** (the "Strand") runs 22 miles along the coast from Will Rogers State Beach in Pacific Palisades to Torrance Beach — uninterrupted, flat, car-free. Renting a bike in Santa Monica and riding south to Venice and Manhattan Beach is one of the best free activities in LA.

**Lime and Bird** e-scooters are available throughout the Westside and Hollywood. Cost: $0.15–0.35/minute depending on the model. Useful for the "last mile" from Metro station to destination.

**Metro Bike Share** operates stations near most Metro rail stops. $2/trip for 30-minute rides.

## Practical Tips

**Download before you arrive**: Google Maps (works offline), the TAP app, Uber/Lyft. The Metro's own app is useful for trip planning.

**Traffic apps**: Waze is marginally better than Google Maps for real-time LA traffic in our experience. Keep both.

**Bus**: LA Metro operates an extensive bus network that is slower than rail but covers many areas the rail system doesn't. The **Rapid** bus lines (indicated by an R prefix) make limited stops. The **720** Rapid along Wilshire Boulevard is a useful east-west connection. Bus rides cost $1.75 with TAP, same as Metro rail.

**Avoid**: The 405 between LAX and the 101 between 3–7pm. The 10 east of downtown between 4–7pm. Surface streets on Sunset and Santa Monica Boulevards between 5–7pm on weekdays. These are reliable enough to plan around.
