Lincoln Financial Field (known locally as "the Linc") opened in 2003 as the home of the Philadelphia Eagles NFL franchise. Located in the South Philadelphia sports complex alongside Citizens Bank Park (the Phillies' baseball stadium) and the Wells Fargo Center (76ers/Flyers), it sits at the terminus of the SEPTA Broad Street Line — making it one of the more transit-accessible major stadiums in the US.

Philadelphia hosting a World Cup semifinal makes this one of the highest-profile venues in the tournament.

---

## Stadium Facts

**Address**: One Lincoln Financial Field Way, Philadelphia, PA 19148  
**Capacity**: 69,176  
**Surface**: Natural grass (replaced between NFL seasons)  
**Roof**: Open-air; partial canopy over upper tier sections  
**World Cup matches**: 6 matches including 1 semifinal  
**Home team**: Philadelphia Eagles (NFL)

---

## Getting There

### SEPTA Broad Street Line (Recommended)

The **Broad Street Line** is the primary transit route — a direct subway connection from Center City to the sports complex.

**Key stations**:
- **City Hall** (Center City): 15–18 minutes to AT&T Station
- **Ellsworth-Federal**: 10 minutes
- **AT&T Station** (sports complex): Adjacent to Lincoln Financial Field

**Fare**: $2.50 one-way. SEPTA Key card (the regional transit card) is the most convenient payment method.

**Match-day service**: SEPTA runs enhanced Broad Street Line service on major event days — trains every 4–6 minutes. AT&T Station handles very large match crowds; the platform is designed for it.

**Return**: The Broad Street Line remains the fastest post-match option. Post-game crowds mean a 10–20 minute wait at AT&T Station before boarding; the line moves efficiently once you board.

### Walking from Center City

Approximately 3 miles from City Hall — a 50–60 minute walk. Not practical in July heat (28–32°C), but fans have done it. Walk south on Broad Street to the stadium complex.

### Driving

I-95 south from Center City, exit at Pattison Avenue. Multiple parking lots in the sports complex and surrounding streets.
- Parking: $25–45 event-day lot; $15–20 in off-site lots with shuttle
- Post-match congestion on I-95 and Broad Street can extend exit time by 45–60 minutes

## The Stadium Environment

**Philadelphia Eagles context**: Eagles fans have one of the most passionate (and occasionally intense) fanbases in American professional sports. The stadium has genuine crowd atmosphere built over decades of NFL games. World Cup crowds will add international diversity to that base.

**The open-air question**: Lincoln Financial Field has a partial roof canopy over upper sections but is largely open-air. July in Philadelphia is hot and humid (28–32°C, afternoon thunderstorm probability). Afternoon World Cup matches may be uncomfortable for sun-exposed seating; evening matches are better.

**Sightlines**: Good throughout the bowl. The 50-yard-line premium sections are covered; end zone and upper tier sections have the least shelter.

---

## The South Philly Sports Complex

The Linc shares a campus with Citizens Bank Park (Phillies baseball) and the Wells Fargo Center. On match days:

**The Xfinity Live! complex** (1100 Pattison Avenue): A permanent entertainment and dining complex built into the sports complex — multiple bars and restaurants with broadcast screens, live music, and the capacity to handle pre-match crowds. The most organized pre-match infrastructure of any Philadelphia venue. Arrive 2+ hours before kickoff; the complex fills.

**Citizens Bank Park parking lots**: On non-Phillies days, the baseball stadium lots become FIFA parking. If a Phillies home game coincides with a World Cup match (check schedules), both stadiums will be operating simultaneously — the sports complex becomes extremely congested.

## Inside the Stadium

**Concessions**: The Linc has a good food program by NFL standards. Philadelphia-specific options include cheesesteaks (stadium version, not Pat's or Geno's, but legitimate), soft pretzels with mustard (a Philadelphia tradition), and the Eagles-specific branded items.

**Beer**: $13–16/pint. The craft beer selection at the Linc includes local Philadelphia options.

**Club sections**: The Hyundai Club and other premium areas are accessible with premium World Cup ticket categories. Air-conditioned space is a genuine advantage for July matches.

---

## The Semifinal Context

Lincoln Financial Field's World Cup semifinal means one of the final four teams plays here. Semifinal tickets command premium prices ($400–800+ face value); resale market prices will be multiples higher. The stadium context for a semifinal — with all four remaining national team supporter groups traveling — will be one of the loudest environments at the tournament.

Philadelphia hotels will be sold out at 3–5x normal rates during semifinal week. Book accommodation months in advance.

---

## Practical Tips

- The Broad Street Line is the correct way to the stadium. $2.50 each way; no traffic; no parking. Buy a SEPTA Key card at Market Street or City Hall stations.
- July afternoon heat is real in Philadelphia. For afternoon kickoffs (2pm, 3pm), wear sunscreen and carry water. Hydration stations are at the stadium.
- The Xfinity Live! complex is the best pre-match option but fills 90 minutes before kickoff. For the semifinal, arrive 3 hours early.
- The Eagles fan culture is intense by NFL standards. For the World Cup, the crowd will be international — but the stadium's reputation for crowd noise is deserved and will apply.
