Lumen Field opened in 2002 as the home of the Seattle Seahawks NFL franchise and has since also become the home of the Seattle Sounders, the most successful MLS franchise by supporter culture. The stadium's enclosed design — partially roofed, with the stands angled to concentrate crowd noise — creates one of the loudest atmospheres in American football. For World Cup 2026, the enclosed bowl and passionate Seattle soccer fanbase creates a World Cup match atmosphere with a distinctive Pacific Northwest character.

## Stadium Facts

**Address**: 800 Occidental Ave S, Seattle, WA 98134  
**Capacity**: 68,740  
**Surface**: Field Turf (artificial) — may be replaced with natural grass for World Cup  
**Roof**: Partial — the upper tier canopy covers most seats from rain  
**World Cup matches**: 6 matches  
**Home teams**: Seattle Seahawks (NFL), Seattle Sounders FC (MLS)

## Getting There

### Link Light Rail (Recommended)

The 1 Line (Central Link) has a **Stadium Station** directly adjacent to Lumen Field — the station is between the stadium and T-Mobile Park (the Mariners baseball stadium). Walk time from station to stadium gate: 3–5 minutes.

**From downtown Seattle** (Westlake Center): 5 minutes, $2.75.  
**From Capitol Hill**: 8 minutes, $2.75.  
**From Sea-Tac Airport**: 38 minutes, $3.25. You can go directly from the airport to the stadium without changing.

**Match day service**: Sound Transit runs enhanced service on match days — trains every 6 minutes during peak match demand. Post-match, trains queue at Stadium Station to handle the crowd; expect 10–20 minutes after the final whistle.

**Buy on the app**: ORCA Card (the regional transit card) works on Link Light Rail. Load it at airport ticket machines or transit center kiosks.

### Walking from Downtown

15–20 minutes south from Pioneer Square on Occidental Avenue South. The walk passes through the industrial SODO district; it's safe and direct.

### Rideshare

Designated pickup zones are several blocks from the stadium — Uber and Lyft post the exact zone location in the app. Post-match surge: $20–45 for downtown routes.

### Driving

Parking in SODO: $20–45/event. The combination of Light Rail efficiency and post-match traffic makes transit the correct choice.

## The Stadium Experience

**The noise**: Lumen Field's canopy and angled bowl concentrate crowd sound. The stadium held a verified Guinness World Record for the loudest outdoor stadium crowd noise in 2013 (and recaptured it in 2014). World Cup matches with passionate supporter groups will be loud.

**Rain**: The partial roof covers most seats from direct rain — the lower bowl has more protection than the upper deck. July in Seattle is typically dry (see the main Seattle guide), but rain remains possible.

**Views**: The sight lines from all sections are good. The south end zone has a large video board; the enclosed bowl means there are no bad sightlines from distance.

**Gates**: Multiple entry points around the perimeter. FIFA will assign gates by ticket section. Arrive 60–90 minutes before kickoff to avoid security queues.

## The SODO Neighborhood

SODO (South of Downtown) is an industrial neighborhood with warehouses, manufacturing, and the stadium district. Not a tourist neighborhood in the conventional sense, but the pre-match environment has its own character:

**Damn the Weather** (116 1st Ave S, Pioneer Square adjacent): A Pioneer Square bar with good draft beer selection — 15-minute walk from the stadium, the best pre-match bar in accessible range.

**The SODO bars**: Several bars have opened to serve stadium crowds on the blocks immediately west and north of Lumen Field. Check for pop-up operations specifically for World Cup events.

**T-Mobile Park** (the Mariners baseball stadium) is adjacent to Lumen Field — on match days without baseball conflicts, the connecting plaza between the two stadiums becomes informal gathering space.

## Inside the Stadium

**Concessions**: PNW seafood at a stadium — Dungeness crab, smoked salmon, fish and chips. Seattle's stadium food is better than most NFL venues because the local food culture demands it. The chowder and the crab are the specific items worth the stadium price premium.

**Beer**: The Pacific Northwest brewery scene means stadium beer selections include Redhook, Pyramid, and local craft options alongside the standard macrobrews. $12–15.

**Merchandise**: World Cup official merchandise at stadiums; team souvenirs.

## Practical Tips

- Link Light Rail from anywhere in the city is the dominant correct choice. The train time from Westlake (downtown) to Stadium Station is 5 minutes; from Capitol Hill is 8 minutes.
- July in Seattle: bring a light jacket. Lumen Field is partially covered but the evening temperatures drop to 15–18°C even in summer.
- The Sounders supporter culture is strong — expect organized supporter group sections and coordinated chanting. Seattle has one of the most engaged MLS fanbases and that culture will carry into World Cup matches.
- Mobile tickets: Lumen Field uses mobile entry. Have the FIFA ticket app loaded before arriving — stadium WiFi is functional but crowded during entry.
