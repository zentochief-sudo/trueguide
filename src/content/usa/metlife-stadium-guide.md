MetLife Stadium is in East Rutherford, New Jersey — seven miles and a world apart from Midtown Manhattan. It is a large, open-air stadium built for the climate extremes of the New York–New Jersey region. In June it can be pleasant; in July it can be hot and humid with afternoon thunderstorm risk. It has excellent transit access via NJ Transit from Penn Station, excellent sightlines from most seating areas, and the distinction of hosting both the opening match and the final of World Cup 2026.

Understanding how the stadium and its transit connections actually work will determine whether your match-day experience is smooth or stressful.

---

## The Venue

**Address**: 1 MetLife Stadium Drive, East Rutherford, NJ 07073

MetLife Stadium opened in 2010 as a joint facility for the New York Giants and New York Jets. Its standard capacity is 82,500, with FIFA expansions bringing this to approximately 87,000–92,000 for the World Cup. It is an open-air stadium — no roof, no dome — which means the New Jersey summer weather (heat, humidity, potential storms) is a factor at every match.

The stadium is part of the **Meadowlands Sports Complex**, which also includes the Meadowlands Racetrack and extensive parking infrastructure. The complex sits in the New Jersey Meadowlands, a low-lying wetland area west of Manhattan across the Hudson River.

**Seating layout**: The lower bowl (100 sections) is close to the pitch, with field-level seats offering an immersive view of pace and physicality. The upper deck (300 sections) provides a broader tactical view of the entire pitch. The club level (200 sections) is the premium tier with climate-controlled interior access.

**The FIFA Final and Opening Match**: Both the opening match and the Final of World Cup 2026 are scheduled for MetLife. If you have a Final ticket, expect maximum security, maximum crowds, and maximum atmosphere. The NJ Transit train becomes the only practical option — the road system around the stadium cannot handle a Final-night crowd trying to leave simultaneously.

## Getting There

### NJ Transit Train (Recommended)

This is the primary transit option and the one that works consistently on match days.

**Step 1**: Get to New York Penn Station (34th Street). Accessible from most Manhattan neighborhoods by any of: 1/2/3 trains (31st–34th Street station), A/C/E trains (34th St–Penn Station), LIRR commuter rail, Amtrak, or NJ Transit commuter rail from Newark/New Jersey.

**Step 2**: Buy NJ Transit tickets. Do this via the **NJ Transit app** before match day. The Penn Station ticket windows and machines have 20–40 minute queues on match days. In-app purchase eliminates this entirely. The Meadowlands Sports Complex station requires a specially ticketed event service — look for "Meadowlands Special" or similar designation on the app on match days.

**Step 3**: Board the designated match-day train. Platforms are announced at Penn Station. The journey takes 20–25 minutes.

**Step 4**: Walk from Meadowlands station to stadium. The station is approximately 0.4 miles from the stadium entrance — an 8–10 minute walk across the parking lot complex. Signage is clear; the stadium is visible from the platform.

**Cost**: $7–10 each way for match-day NJ Transit service. Budget $20–22 total for round-trip transit on a match day.

**Return trip**: After the final whistle, the station fills immediately. If you can wait 20–30 minutes in the stadium concourse or at the complex's food vendors, the queue shortens dramatically. Trains run frequently post-match.

### Port Authority Bus Terminal

Express bus service operates from the Port Authority Bus Terminal (8th Avenue and 41st Street) directly to MetLife on match days. Journey time: 30–45 minutes depending on New Jersey traffic. Cost: similar to NJ Transit, approximately $8–12 each way.

The bus is a viable alternative if your hotel is closer to Times Square than to Penn Station, or if the NJ Transit train is sold out on a specific match day (rare, but possible for the Final and knockout rounds).

### Rideshare

Uber and Lyft are available, but match-day surge pricing from Midtown to MetLife runs $65–110 each way. If you're driving in from a New Jersey location (Newark, Hoboken, Jersey City), rideshare is more cost-effective — budget $20–35 from Newark, $25–40 from Hoboken.

Designated rideshare pickup/dropoff is on the eastern side of the stadium complex via Paterson Plank Road. GPS will route you here; tell the driver "MetLife rideshare" rather than "MetLife Stadium main entrance" to avoid the pedestrian-only zone confusion.

### Driving

If you're staying in New Jersey or arriving from Connecticut via I-95, driving to MetLife is viable for non-Final matches.

Parking: $35–60 pre-purchased (online, weeks ahead). On-the-day prices will be higher. Parking lots are extensive — this is a stadium designed around car access. The Lot H entrance from NJ Route 3 West is typically the smoothest inbound approach.

Post-match: Expect 45–75 minutes to exit the parking area and reach a moving highway after any full-capacity match. The northbound Route 3 toward the Lincoln Tunnel backs up significantly. If you're heading to New Jersey destinations, Route 120 North toward I-95 is often faster than fighting for the Lincoln Tunnel.

## Stadium Arrival

**When to arrive**: NJ Transit service typically begins 2.5–3 hours before kickoff. Arrive at the stadium 90 minutes before kickoff for regular group stage matches, 2 hours for knockout stages and the Final. Security screening for 87,000 people moves quickly but requires time.

**Clear bag policy**: All bags must be clear. Single clear bags up to 12" × 6" × 12" or a small wristlet/clutch (4.5" × 6.5"). Backpacks (even clear) may be subject to additional screening. Diaper bags and medical bags are exceptions — check the official World Cup ticketing site for current rules.

**Tickets**: Digital tickets on your phone are preferred. Download your ticket to the device's wallet so it works offline. Print a backup — the NJ Transit tunnel areas have inconsistent cell coverage.

**What to bring**:
- Light layers — MetLife is open-air and evening temperatures in July can be 22–26°C, cooler than afternoon
- Compact umbrella or rain jacket (July afternoon thunderstorms are possible)
- Sunscreen (afternoon matches have direct sun exposure in the lower bowl)
- Water (sealed bottles permitted up to 1 liter; stadium water fountains are available)

## Food and Drink

MetLife Stadium has improved its food program substantially since opening, though it still operates on an NFL gameday model rather than a European stadium food culture.

**Standard options**: Hot dogs, pretzels, nachos, burgers ($15–22). Available at all concourse levels.

**World Cup additions**: FIFA match-day concessions typically expand the food program to reflect the host city and tournament atmosphere. Expect Latin American street food options (arepas, empanadas, food from the competing nations' cuisines), international beer brands alongside American options, and premium food areas in the club sections.

**Alcohol**: Beer from $15–17 (16oz). Cocktails $18–25. FIFA World Cup alcohol policies vary by tournament — check match-day guidance, as rules may restrict sales in seating areas while permitting them in concourses.

**Pre-match at the complex**: The Meadowlands Racing complex adjacent to the stadium has bars and food vendors that operate on match days. These fill early for large matches — the Final crowd will have the entire complex packed by 3 hours before kickoff.

## Weather at MetLife

MetLife Stadium is open-air, and the New Jersey summer climate is meaningfully different from [SoFi Stadium](/united-states/sofi-stadium-guide) in LA or Hard Rock in Miami.

**June**: 23–28°C, humidity 60–70%, afternoon thunderstorm probability 20–30%. Thunderstorms at MetLife can cause match delays — FIFA has protocols for lightning within 8 miles of the stadium (a 30-minute hold). Bring a compact rain jacket.

**July**: 26–32°C, higher humidity, heat index 32–38°C during afternoon matches. Evening matches (kickoff after 6pm local) are significantly more comfortable. If your match kicks off at 3pm in July, wear lightweight, moisture-wicking clothing and plan for heat.

**The Final**: Scheduled for evening kickoff (exact time TBD). July evening temperatures at MetLife are typically 23–27°C — comfortable for football, excellent for atmosphere.

## Accessibility

MetLife Stadium has extensive accessibility infrastructure:
- Accessible entrances at all eight gate locations
- Elevator access to all levels
- Accessible seating with companion seats across all price tiers
- Sensory inclusion rooms (quiet spaces away from main stadium noise)
- Braille materials and hearing loop systems
- Accessible parking in designated lots (pre-purchase required via official stadium site)

The main accessible entrance is Gate C on the east side of the stadium. Guest services is located at each gate and on the main concourse at Section 119.

## Around the Stadium

The Meadowlands Sports Complex does not have much in the way of restaurants and bars outside the match-day setup. The practical pre-match food strategy is to eat in Manhattan before taking the train — Penn Station is surrounded by food options, and arriving at MetLife 90 minutes before kickoff is sufficient without requiring a full pre-match restaurant meal at the complex.

If you want to be at the stadium for the atmosphere: the Team Experience areas and FIFA Fan Zone around the stadium perimeter typically open 3–4 hours before kickoff. These areas have entertainment, food, and the pre-match atmosphere that makes World Cup day-of logistics feel like the event itself rather than just transit.
