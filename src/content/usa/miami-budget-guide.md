Miami is expensive, but not uniformly so. The city has a genuine split between the tourist-facing South Beach economy (where everything costs twice what it should) and the Latin American community economy (where a full plate of ropa vieja with rice and black beans costs $14 and is excellent). Navigating between these two worlds is the main financial skill a World Cup visitor needs.

The World Cup will push accommodation prices 40–80% above normal summer rates on match weeks. Book early. Everything else — food, transport, beaches — has an affordable version within easy reach.

---

## Accommodation

**Hostels**: $40–70/night for a dorm bed. The hostel market in Miami Beach is thinner than in NYC or LA. **Freehand Miami** (2727 Indian Creek Drive, Miami Beach) is the best-known option — a converted historic hotel with shared and private rooms, a pool, and one of the better lobby bars in the city. **HI Miami Beach** is the traditional budget option.

**Budget hotels**: $130–200/night for the lower end of Miami Beach properties. South Beach budget hotels are often older properties with limited amenities — acceptable for a trip focused on the beach and nightlife, less so if you want a comfortable base. Brickell and Wynwood offer better quality at this price point.

**Mid-range hotels**: $210–320/night in normal summer conditions. Match-week premiums push this to $280–480 in South Beach. The same budget goes significantly further in Brickell ($180–280/night) or Wynwood ($160–260/night).

**Airbnb**: More viable in Miami than in NYC (fewer regulatory restrictions). A private room in a South Beach apartment runs $90–150/night. A two-bedroom apartment for a group of four: $180–280/night total (split, that's $45–70 per person) — the best budget accommodation strategy for groups.

**Fort Lauderdale as a base**: Hotels in Fort Lauderdale run $120–200/night for mid-range properties — significantly cheaper than Miami Beach. Fort Lauderdale has its own beach (Las Olas Boulevard), Tri-Rail connection to Miami, and proximity to Hard Rock Stadium. A legitimate base option for budget-conscious visitors willing to trade South Beach's nightlife for lower costs.

## Getting Around

**Rideshare**: The main transport cost variable. Standard fares within South Beach or Brickell: $10–18. Cross-city trips (South Beach to Wynwood): $12–20. Match-day Hard Rock surge: $65–100 each way.

**Metrorail**: $2.25/ride. The EASY Card is the tap-to-pay option. For Brickell and Downtown-based visitors, Metrorail handles airport trips ($2.25 vs. $35–50 for rideshare) and Tri-Rail connections for the stadium.

**Citi Bike**: $4.50 per 30 minutes or $19/day unlimited. A day pass is excellent value for visitors who plan to explore South Beach, Wynwood, and Brickell by bike. The $19 covers 10+ short trips that would cost $15–20 total in rideshare.

**Rental car**: $55–100/day plus parking. Useful for Everglades day trips and Coconut Grove visits. Not cost-effective for pure South Beach stays where parking is $20–35/day on top of the car cost.

## Food

Miami has the widest range of food price points of any World Cup city.

**$25–35/day eating well**:
- Breakfast: Cuban coffee (cortadito $1.50) and pastelito (guava pastry $1.50–2) from a Little Havana ventanita or the equivalent in any Miami neighborhood
- Lunch: El Palacio de los Jugos plate ($12–15) or a Cuban sandwich at Versailles ($12)
- Dinner: Ceviche and an arepa at a Venezuelan/Peruvian casual spot ($16–20)

**$70–100/day at actual restaurants**:
- One mid-range dinner per day ($40–55 with a drink)
- Cheap breakfasts and casual lunches

**The splurge tier ($150–200/day food)**:
- One dinner at a serious restaurant (La Mar, KYU in Wynwood, an Argentine parrilla)
- Normal lunches and breakfast

**Where the value is**: Little Havana (Calle Ocho), El Palacio de los Jugos, any Venezuelan or Colombian quick-service operation in Brickell or Doral. **Where to avoid spending money on food**: Ocean Drive, Bayside Marketplace, hotel restaurants.

## Beaches

All public beaches in Florida are free to access. The cost comes from parking.

**South Beach parking**: $2–4/hour metered street parking. The 7th and 13th Street garages: $25–35/day. Municipal lots: $5–15/day at some distances.

**Beat parking costs**: Take Citi Bike, rideshare (practical given South Beach's small geographic size), or stay at a hotel within walking distance of the beach.

**North of South Beach**: Surfside, Bal Harbour, and Haulover Beach all have free or low-cost beach access with better parking ratios than South Beach.

## Nightlife

Nightlife in Miami is the biggest potential budget buster. The correct expectation:

**Bars (normal)**: $12–18 for a cocktail in Wynwood or Brickell. $15–22 in South Beach hotel bars. $20–28 at premium South Beach venues.

**Club entry**: Free for early arrivals (before midnight) at many Wynwood venues. $30–60 for South Beach clubs on weekends. Premium event nights: $80–150. Bottle service (the model many South Beach clubs push): $400–2,000 for a table minimum.

**The budget nightlife strategy**: Wynwood bars and Brickell bars on weekdays, South Beach on one designated night budgeted as a splurge. The $30 Wynwood bar night (four drinks, no entry fee) versus the $150 South Beach club night (entry plus two cocktails) represent the same city, very different experiences.

## Tipping

Florida operates on standard US tipping culture plus one local variable:

- **Sit-down restaurants**: 18–20% standard. Some South Beach restaurants auto-add 18–20% gratuity to tables of 5+ — check the bill before adding more.
- **Beach service** (loungers, umbrellas): $2–5 tip to the attendant is standard.
- **Hotel pool service**: $2–3 per round of drinks.
- **Rideshare**: 15–20% in-app.
- **Bars**: $1–2 per drink.

Budget approximately 20% on all food, drink, and service spending for tips.

## Sample Daily Budgets

**Budget traveler ($110–135/day)**:
- Hostel (Freehand or similar): $65
- Food: $30 (Cuban food, ventanita coffee, informal lunch)
- Transport: $10 (Metrorail + Citi Bike + minimal rideshare)
- Beach: $0 (free access, walk or bike from South Beach hotel)
- Tips: $6
- Match day add-on: +$70–100 for rideshare to Hard Rock
- **Total: ~$111** non-match days

**Mid-range traveler ($250–300/day)**:
- Hotel (South Beach mid-range): $200
- Food: $70 (one restaurant, other meals casual)
- Transport: $20 (Citi Bike + rideshare)
- Attraction/beach: $15
- Tips: $14
- **Total: ~$319** — South Beach mid-range is expensive

**Budget smart move**: Stay in Wynwood or Brickell ($160–200 hotel), eat Cuban and Venezuelan food, use Citi Bike and Metrorail, reserve the rideshare budget for match days. Same city experience at $170–210/day.

## World Cup-Specific Costs

**Match ticket**: Group stage $100–300; knockouts $200–600+. Official FIFA ticketing only.

**Match-day transport**: Round-trip rideshare surge from South Beach = $130–200. Round-trip Tri-Rail via Opa-locka (if using FLL hotel base) = $12–14. The transport differential between a South Beach base and a Fort Lauderdale base over three match days is $300–550.

**Practical total**: A 7-night World Cup trip with 2–3 matches, South Beach hotel, mid-range food, and reasonable nightlife budget runs $2,500–4,000 per person (not including flights and match tickets). The same trip from a Fort Lauderdale or Brickell base: $1,800–2,800.
