NRG Stadium in Houston is the only World Cup venue with a retractable roof that is specifically designed for Houston summer conditions — which is to say, conditions that would otherwise make outdoor football at 3pm in July a serious health concern. The dome stays closed for summer matches, the industrial HVAC keeps the interior at approximately 22°C, and the 72,220-seat capacity (expandable to ~80,000 for the World Cup) fills with the most ethnically diverse crowd of any stadium in the United States.

The transit story at NRG is the best of any Texas World Cup venue: the METRORail Red Line stops directly at the stadium. No shuttle. No transfer. Downtown to the gate in 20 minutes.

---

## The Venue

**Address**: 1 NRG Park, Houston, TX 77054

NRG Stadium opened in 2002 as Reliant Stadium, co-located with the historic Astrodome (the world's first domed stadium, now a protected landmark) in the NRG Park complex. The current stadium has a retractable roof, excellent sightlines from all sections due to a steep lower bowl design, and a consistent reputation as one of the better game-day experiences in the NFL.

**The roof**: Retractable, made of translucent panels that allow filtered natural light when closed. For World Cup summer matches, the roof will be closed and the HVAC will run throughout the match. Temperature inside: approximately 22–24°C regardless of external conditions.

**Seating**: The lower bowl is steeper than most American stadiums — good for sightlines but noticeable for vertigo-prone visitors in the upper sections. The club level (200s) has the widest seats and the best views for watching team shape and tactical structure.

**The Astrodome**: The original dome adjacent to NRG Stadium is a designated Houston landmark currently undergoing renovation. Its distinctive circular profile is visible from the stadium parking areas. If it is open for visits during the World Cup, worth 30 minutes of your time for the architectural history.

## Getting There

### METRORail Red Line (Recommended)

The Houston METRORail Red Line has a dedicated station at **NRG Park/Astrodome** adjacent to the stadium entrance. This is the clearest direct transit connection of any Texas World Cup venue.

**From downtown (Main Street Square station)**: 15–20 minutes. Trains run every 6–12 minutes during peak hours. $1.25 with Q card or cash. Match-day service increases frequency.

**From Museum District (Museum District station)**: 10 minutes. $1.25.

**From Texas Medical Center area**: 5 minutes. $1.25.

**Getting a Q card**: Available at any METRORail station machine ($1.50 card fee). Load $10–15. Each trip is $1.25. Contactless payment also accepted at newer validators.

**Practical**: The NRG station is a 3–5 minute walk from the main stadium entrance. Signs are clear. The walkway is covered for the final stretch.

**Return journey**: Post-match trains are frequent and the platform queue moves steadily. The rail avoids all post-match vehicle traffic entirely. Expect 10–20 minutes at the platform before boarding during the immediate post-match period.

### Rideshare

$15–25 from downtown under normal conditions. Match-day surge: $35–60 from downtown. The transit option is dramatically better value.

Designated rideshare zone is on the south side of the complex, accessed via NRG Parkway. Post-match rideshare from NRG is faster to arrange than from AT&T Stadium in Arlington because the road network around NRG is better connected.

### Driving

- **From downtown Houston**: I-69/US-59 South to Kirby Drive exit, south to NRG Parkway. 15–20 minutes.
- **From I-610 (the Loop)**: Exit Main Street South or Kirby Drive South.

Parking: $20–40 pre-purchased. The NRG Park complex has approximately 30,000 spaces. Lots A and B (premium, closest) are $40; Lots C–F are $20–25. The walk from the outer lots is 10–15 minutes.

Post-match: The freeway network around NRG is better connected than Arlington's situation. Expect 20–40 minutes to reach a free-flowing highway.

## Stadium Arrival

**When to arrive**: 90 minutes before kickoff (regular group stage), 2 hours for knockout rounds. The stadium processes large crowds efficiently but the additional World Cup security screening adds time.

**Clear bag policy**: Standard clear bag (12" × 6" × 12") or small clutch. No backpacks. Medical and diaper bag exceptions apply.

**Tickets**: Download the QR code to your phone wallet. NRG's cellular coverage inside the dome is generally good, but having a screenshot or wallet-stored version is the safe approach.

**Temperature**: Inside: 22–24°C (air-conditioned dome). Outside en route to the stadium: 34–38°C in July. Dress for the outdoor walk, bring a light layer for the stadium interior if you run cold in AC.

## Food and Drink

NRG Stadium's food program reflects Houston's food culture more directly than most NFL stadiums.

**Texas barbecue**: Brisket sandwiches and plates from Houston-area barbecue vendors. Not at the Pecan Lodge level but legitimate Texas barbecue.

**Vietnamese crawfish / Tex-Mex**: Some concession areas will reflect the [Houston food](/united-states/houston-food-guide) landscape — expect bao, Vietnamese-influenced dishes, and proper Tex-Mex alongside standard stadium fare.

**Seafood**: Gulf shrimp and fish items reflecting Houston's proximity to the Gulf Coast.

**Standard fare**: Hot dogs, nachos, burgers ($14–20).

**Beer**: $14–16 for standard options. Houston craft beers (Saint Arnold, 8th Wonder) at some concourse points.

## The NRG Park Complex

The area around NRG Stadium has more infrastructure than AT&T Stadium's Arlington context.

**NRG Center**: The adjacent convention center has pre-match events, FIFA Fan Zones, and vendor areas on match days.

**The Astrodome (pending renovation status)**: The 1965 original dome is adjacent and is being considered for adaptive reuse as a hotel or event space. If open during the World Cup, the architecture alone — the first climate-controlled domed sports venue in history — is worth an hour.

**Restaurant Row**: Several restaurants on Kirby Drive and Main Street South (5–10 minutes from the stadium) are practical for pre-match meals. The neighborhoods adjacent to the Medical Center corridor have excellent Vietnamese and Latin American food options within a short rideshare.

## Accessibility

- Accessible entrances at all eight gate locations
- Elevator access to all levels
- Accessible seating throughout with companion seats
- Hearing loop systems and assistive listening devices
- Sensory-inclusive room on Level 2
- Accessible parking in Lot A (pre-purchase required)

Guest services located at Gates 1, 4, and 8 on the main concourse level.
