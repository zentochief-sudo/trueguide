New York City has the most comprehensive public transit system in North America. The subway runs 24 hours, the bus network covers every neighborhood, and the ferry system offers scenic alternatives along the waterfront. For World Cup visitors, two transit systems matter most: the NYC Subway for moving around the five boroughs, and NJ Transit for reaching MetLife Stadium in New Jersey.

Understanding how they work — and where they fail — will save you significant time and money.

---

## The NYC Subway

The subway is 472 stations across 245 route miles, operating 24 hours, 365 days a year. It is imperfect, occasionally delayed, sometimes confusing, and irreplaceable.

**How to pay**: 

The **OMNY** contactless system allows you to tap any contactless credit card, debit card, Apple Pay, or Google Pay. Each tap is $2.90. An 11-ride OMNY discount activates automatically if you exceed $34 in taps in a week (equivalent to a 7-day unlimited pass).

Alternatively, buy a **MetroCard** at any station vending machine. A 7-day unlimited pass costs $34. A single-ride MetroCard is $3.25 (more expensive than OMNY tap). For any stay of 3+ days with regular subway use, the 7-day unlimited is the obvious choice.

**Key lines for World Cup visitors**:

| Line | Color | Key stations |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 1/2/3 | Red | Penn Station (34th), Times Square, 14th St, Chambers St |
| A/C/E | Blue | JFK (A train), Penn Station, Times Square, World Trade Center |
| N/Q/R/W | Yellow | Times Square, Rockefeller Ctr, 14th St Union Sq, Atlantic Ave Brooklyn |
| 4/5/6 | Green | Grand Central, Union Square, Brooklyn Bridge, Bronx |
| L | Grey | Williamsburg (Bedford Ave) to Union Square (14th St) |
| 7 | Purple | Queens (Flushing, Jackson Heights) to Times Square |
| B/D/F/M | Orange | Midtown, West Village, Brooklyn (Prospect Park, Atlantic Ave) |

**The 24-hour advantage**: Unlike nearly every other major city's subway system, New York's runs through the night. This changes the economics of nightlife — you don't need to leave before the last train or budget for a late-night taxi. The subway at 2am is slower (express lines run local overnight) but functional.

**Delays and service changes**: Weekend service frequently runs modified patterns due to maintenance work. Check the MTA website (mta.info) or the Citymapper app for real-time service alerts. The subway app "Subway Time" gives accurate real-time arrival predictions.

## Getting to MetLife Stadium

MetLife Stadium is in East Rutherford, New Jersey — connected to Manhattan via the NJ Transit rail system.

### NJ Transit (Primary Route)

**Step 1 — Get to Penn Station**: Take any subway to 34th Street–Penn Station (1/2/3 trains or A/C/E trains) or walk from your hotel if you're staying in Midtown.

**Step 2 — Buy NJ Transit tickets**: Download the **NJ Transit app** and buy match-day event tickets before you leave your hotel. The app shows "Meadowlands Special" or similar event-specific train service on match days. Cost: $7–10 each way.

Do NOT wait until you arrive at Penn Station to buy tickets. The ticket counter and machine queues on match days are 20–40 minutes long.

**Step 3 — Board the train**: Match-day trains depart from Penn Station platforms announced on the departure boards. The journey to Meadowlands Sports Complex station takes 20–25 minutes.

**Step 4 — Walk to stadium**: From the Meadowlands station, the stadium is approximately 0.4 miles (8–10 minute walk). The route is clear; the stadium is visible.

**Return journey**: After the final whistle, the station fills immediately. Trains run frequently but the initial queue is 15–30 minutes. If you wait 20–25 minutes post-match at the stadium concourse or complex food area, the queue halves.

### Bus from Port Authority

Express buses depart from the Port Authority Bus Terminal (8th Avenue & 41st Street) directly to MetLife on match days. Journey time: 30–45 minutes. Cost: $8–12 each way. 

The bus is slightly slower than the train and requires navigating Port Authority's confusing bus terminal, but it is a genuine alternative if NJ Transit trains are sold out for a specific match (this is more likely for the Final and later knockout rounds).

### Car and Rideshare

Driving is practical from New Jersey hotels (Hoboken, Jersey City, Newark) or for visitors who prefer it.

- **From Midtown Manhattan via rideshare**: $65–110 surge pricing on match days each way. Only makes sense if you're in a group splitting the cost.
- **From Newark**: $20–35 rideshare, or NJ Transit from Newark Penn Station (15 minutes, $5–7).
- **Driving**: Route 3 West from the Lincoln Tunnel, or Route 120 North from I-95 if coming from the south. Parking $40–60 pre-purchased. Post-match, expect 45–75 minutes to exit the complex and reach a moving highway.

## Getting from Airports

### JFK Airport

**AirTrain + A train (recommended)**: AirTrain from terminal to Howard Beach station ($8.50), then A train to Midtown (~50 minutes from Howard Beach, $2.90). Total: ~65–75 minutes, ~$11.40. Buy your subway fare at the Howard Beach station vending machine, where you can combine it with the AirTrain purchase.

**AirTrain + Jamaica + LIRR**: Faster (50–55 minutes) but more complex and more expensive. Take the AirTrain to Jamaica, board LIRR to Penn Station. Cost: $8.50 (AirTrain) + $12–22 (LIRR depending on peak/off-peak) = $20–30.

**Rideshare**: $45–80 base (plus surge), 45–75 minutes depending on traffic. Book in advance for arrival times.

### Newark Liberty Airport (EWR)

**AirTrain + NJ Transit** (recommended): Take the free AirTrain to Newark Penn Station (10 minutes), then NJ Transit to New York Penn Station (30 minutes, $7–12). Total door-to-door: 50–60 minutes. This also positions you perfectly for the MetLife NJ Transit connection.

**PATH train**: From Newark Penn Station, take PATH to 33rd Street Manhattan ($2.75). Journey time similar to NJ Transit. PATH runs 24 hours; NJ Transit has reduced overnight service.

**Rideshare**: $45–75 from Newark to Midtown under normal conditions. Significantly faster than JFK rideshare.

### LaGuardia Airport (LGA)

LGA is the only major New York airport without a direct rail connection. Options:

**Q70 bus**: Free bus from LGA to Jackson Heights–Roosevelt Ave subway station (7, E, F, M, R trains), then subway to Midtown. Total: 35–55 minutes, costs only the subway fare ($2.90).

**M60 bus**: From LGA to Astoria, connecting to the N/W train. Less direct than the Q70.

**Rideshare**: $25–55 to Midtown, 30–60 minutes depending on time of day. LGA is much closer to Midtown than JFK — this is the most practical airport for Manhattan-based visitors if you have flexibility in booking flights.

## Taxis and Rideshare

NYC yellow taxis are metered: $3.00 base fare + $0.70/minute or $2.60/mile. In Midtown traffic, the meter moves primarily on time, not distance. A 2-mile trip in moderate traffic costs $12–18.

Rideshare (Uber/Lyft/Via): Competitive with taxis on price under normal conditions. Note that NYC charges an additional $2.75 congestion surcharge on all rideshare trips in Midtown (south of 96th Street on the East Side, south of 110th Street on the West Side). The subway is almost always faster and cheaper in Manhattan.

## Ferries and Bikes

**NYC Ferry**: Runs routes along the East River and Upper New York Bay. Useful for getting from Brooklyn waterfront areas (DUMBO, Red Hook, Astoria) to Midtown. Cost: $4 per ride. Slower than the subway but scenic. The East River Ferry from Williamsburg to 34th Street takes 25 minutes.

**Citi Bike**: The NYC bike share program has 25,000+ bikes at 1,500+ stations across Manhattan and Brooklyn. $4.50 for a 30-minute ride; $19/day for unlimited 30-minute rides. Excellent for short trips (1–3 miles) where the subway walk time approaches the bike ride time. The protected lanes on 9th Avenue and along the waterfront are safe and pleasant.

**Walking**: New York is a walking city within neighborhoods. The crosstown distance of Manhattan at 42nd Street is about 0.9 miles; uptown/downtown distances are measured in blocks (approximately 20 blocks = 1 mile). Walk the distance when it's under 1.5 miles and you have time — the street life is part of the experience.

## Apps to Download

**Citymapper**: Best overall NYC transit navigation. Shows subway, bus, bike, and walking routes with real-time updates. Essential.

**MTA app or Subway Time**: Real-time subway arrivals.

**NJ Transit**: Book match-day train tickets to MetLife here. Download and register before your trip.

**Citi Bike**: For bike share rentals.

**Google Maps**: Works well for NYC navigation; slightly less real-time than Citymapper for subway alerts.
