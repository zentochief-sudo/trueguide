Philadelphia is one of the more affordable World Cup cities on the East Coast — meaningfully less expensive than New York, comparable to Boston, and with significantly lower hotel rates than either. The city's underrated food culture creates extraordinary value: Reading Terminal Market meals for $10–16, Italian Market for even less, and cheesesteaks at $13–17. Combined with a SEPTA transit system that runs directly to Lincoln Financial Field for $2.50, Philadelphia provides good value relative to the match experience it offers.

---

## Accommodation

**Budget options**: Hostels ($40–65/night dorm). Budget hotels in South Philadelphia and University City: $110–160/night.

**Mid-range hotels**: $160–280/night in Center City and Old City. Philadelphia's hotel market is below Boston and New York for comparable properties.

**Boutique**: $200–340/night. The Morris House Hotel in Old City and the Loews Philadelphia are the anchors.

**Airbnb**: Fishtown: $100–180/night. South Philly: $90–160/night. Old City: $130–220/night.

**World Cup premium**: 35–55% above base rates on match weeks (higher for the semifinal week — 60–80% above). Book 4+ months ahead for the semifinal.

---

## Transport

**SEPTA subway**: $2.50/ride. Airport to Center City: $6.75 (Airport Line).

**SEPTA Day Pass**: $11 unlimited.

**Broad Street Line to stadium**: $2.50 each way. Round-trip match day transit: $5 — one of the best values among World Cup venues.

**Rideshare**: 
- Airport to Center City: $20–35
- Old City to South Philly (cheesesteak): $12–18
- Post-match surge from stadium: $25–45

## Food

**$25–40/day eating well**:
- Breakfast: Reading Terminal Market pastry or Beiler's donut ($4–8)
- Lunch: DiNic's roast pork at Reading Terminal ($12) or Italian Market vendor ($10–14)
- Dinner: Fishtown neighborhood restaurant ($18–25)

**$75–100/day at restaurants**:
- One restaurant dinner (Zahav or Vernick level: $70–90) plus market meals

**The genuine value case**: Philadelphia's market culture is one of the strongest in the US. Reading Terminal Market lunch for $10–16 is world-class in quality — DiNic's roast pork sandwich won a national best sandwich competition. Eating this well for this price doesn't exist at comparable quality in New York or San Francisco.

## Attractions

**Free**:
- Independence National Historical Park (Independence Hall exterior, Liberty Bell viewing)
- Elfreth's Alley (self-guided walk)
- Philadelphia Museum of Art exterior and steps
- Fairmount Park system (2,000+ acres of parks along the Schuylkill River)
- Mural Arts walking tours (self-guided)

**Paid**:
- Independence Hall tour: $1 (timed entry, book in advance)
- Liberty Bell Center: Free
- Philadelphia Museum of Art: $25 (pay-what-you-wish on the first Sunday of each month)
- Barnes Foundation: $30 (one of the great Impressionist collections in the world)
- Eastern State Penitentiary: $20 (a preserved Gothic Revival prison, one of the most interesting historic sites in Philadelphia)

## Sample Daily Budgets

**Budget traveler ($95–115/day)**:
- Budget hotel or Airbnb (South Philly): $100
- Food: $30 (Reading Terminal, Italian Market, cheesesteak)
- Transport: $5 (SEPTA day pass for 2 rides)
- Attraction: $0 (Elfreth's Alley, Art Museum steps)
- Tips: $6
- **Total: ~$141** — Airbnb in South Philly drops this to ~$110

**Mid-range ($240–290/day)**:
- Mid-range hotel (Center City): $210
- Food: $80 (one restaurant dinner + market meals)
- Transport: $15 (SEPTA + rideshare)
- Attraction: $25 (Barnes Foundation or Eastern State)
- Tips: $16
- **Total: ~$346**

**Match-day transport**: $5 round-trip by Broad Street Line. The best match-day transit deal among the Eastern US World Cup cities.
