Philadelphia's position on the Amtrak Northeast Corridor makes it the most connected World Cup host city for day trips — New York is 90 minutes away by Acela, Washington DC is 90 minutes south, Baltimore is 60 minutes, and the Amtrak trains run frequently. Beyond the urban day trip options, Lancaster County's Amish countryside and the New Jersey Shore beaches are accessible by regional transit.

---

## New York City

**Distance**: 97 miles northeast via Amtrak  
**Train time**: 68–90 minutes (Acela express); 95 minutes (Northeast Regional)  
**Best for**: Full day urban exploration

New York City is the most dramatic day trip from any World Cup host city — the combination of frequency (trains every 30–60 minutes from 30th Street Station), journey time (68 minutes on the Acela), and the scale of the destination makes it genuinely feasible.

**Cost**: Acela express: $50–120 one-way (flexible booking); Northeast Regional: $25–60 one-way. Book in advance for lower fares.

**What to do in NYC from Philadelphia**: The primary consideration is time — with a 90-minute train each way, a 9am departure gets you to Penn Station by 10:30am; returning by 9pm gives you 10 hours. This is enough for: a specific neighborhood (the Whitney Museum and the High Line; Central Park; the Lower East Side); one exceptional restaurant; the [Brooklyn](/united-states/brooklyn-guide) Bridge walk. Not enough for a comprehensive New York visit.

**Practical note**: For World Cup visitors who will also have matches in New York ([MetLife Stadium](/united-states/metlife-stadium-guide)), the reverse day trip logic applies — visit Philadelphia from New York on a non-match day.

## Washington DC

**Distance**: 137 miles southwest via Amtrak  
**Train time**: 80–100 minutes (Acela); 2 hours (Northeast Regional)  
**Best for**: Full day history and museums

Washington DC is the US capital and the city with the most comprehensive set of free world-class museums in the country — the Smithsonian Institution's 19 museums, most of which are on or near the National Mall.

**The essential Washington**: The National Mall runs 2 miles from the Lincoln Memorial to the Capitol. The Smithsonian museums along the Mall include the National Air and Space Museum (the most visited museum in the US), the National Museum of Natural History, the National Museum of American History, and the National Gallery of Art. All free.

The Washington monuments: the Lincoln Memorial, the Vietnam Veterans Memorial Wall, the World War II Memorial, and the Washington Monument (the obelisk).

**Combined with Philadelphia**: Visitors with multiple World Cup matches in both Philadelphia and New York might route through Washington DC between the two cities — the Amtrak Northeast Corridor connects all three.

**Cost**: Acela: $60–140 one-way. Northeast Regional: $30–70.

## Lancaster County (Pennsylvania Dutch Country)

**Distance**: 66 miles west via US-30 or PA Turnpike  
**Train/bus time**: 90 minutes by Amtrak to Lancaster; rideshare for the rural areas  
**Best for**: Half-day rural/cultural

Lancaster County is home to the largest Amish community in the US — approximately 50,000 Old Order Amish who live without electricity, drive horse-drawn buggies, and farm using traditional methods. The landscape is extraordinary: rolling hills, dairy farms, farmhouses, and the horse-and-buggy traffic that makes Lancaster County look unlike any other landscape in the US.

**What to do**:
- **Drive (or rideshare) the back roads**: The rural roads between Intercourse, Bird-in-Hand, Paradise, and Strasburg pass working farms and Amish communities. The names of the Lancaster County towns are a specific category of American place-naming.
- **Amish Farm and House** (2395 Covered Bridge Drive): A guided tour of an 18th-century Amish farmhouse and working farm — the most accessible educational introduction to Amish culture. $14.
- **Central Market** (Center Square, Lancaster city): The oldest publicly owned market in the US (since 1730), open Tuesday and Friday. Local produce, Amish baked goods, and the Lancaster food economy.

**Getting there**: Amtrak from 30th Street Station to Lancaster in 75 minutes ($18–30). The rural area requires a car or rideshare from Lancaster city.

## Cape May, New Jersey

**Distance**: 90 miles south via the Garden State Parkway  
**Driving time**: 2–2.5 hours  
**Best for**: Full day beach and Victorian architecture

Cape May is the southernmost tip of New Jersey — a Victorian resort town with 600 preserved Victorian buildings and beaches on Delaware Bay. It has a different character from the Jersey Shore towns to the north: more historic, quieter, with a specific Victorian-resort identity that has survived because the island's geography limits development.

**What to do**: The Victorian house walking tour (maps at the Welcome Center), the beach (Delaware Bay beaches are calmer than the Atlantic-facing Jersey Shore), the Washington Street Mall (outdoor pedestrian shopping district), and Cape May Point State Park (the lighthouse, the bird migration observatory — one of the best hawk-watching sites on the East Coast).

**Getting there**: Car is the practical option — no direct rail to Cape May from Philadelphia.

## Atlantic City

**Distance**: 60 miles east via the Atlantic City Expressway  
**Train time**: 75 minutes (NJ Transit from 30th Street)  
**Best for**: Half-day beach + casino

Atlantic City is New Jersey's casino resort on the Atlantic coast — a specific American cultural experience (the boardwalk, the casinos, the combination of entertainment and decay) that is worth understanding as context for the broader landscape of American popular culture.

**NJ Transit**: Trains run from 30th Street Station to Atlantic City Terminal. $23–28 round trip. The train arrives steps from the boardwalk.

**The boardwalk**: A 5.5-mile wooden boardwalk along the beach — the model for the Monopoly board game properties (Atlantic City street names are the Monopoly board). The casino hotels dominate the skyline; the beachfront is public.
