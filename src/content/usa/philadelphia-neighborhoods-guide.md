Philadelphia is built on William Penn's 1682 grid — one of the first planned cities in America, with a rational street system (numbered streets running north-south, named streets east-west) that makes it unusually easy to navigate. The neighborhoods each have a distinct character shaped by immigrant history, economic cycles of decline and renewal, and the physical organization of the original plan.

For World Cup visitors, the key question is proximity: Old City has the history and transit access; Center City has the hotels; Fishtown has the best new food and nightlife; South Philadelphia has the Italian heritage and the stadiums.

---

## Old City

Old City is the colonial-era neighborhood between the Delaware River and 6th Street, north of Walnut Street. It contains the highest concentration of American Revolutionary history in the world — Independence Hall, the Liberty Bell Center, Christ Church, Elfreth's Alley — within a 6-block radius.

**What's here beyond the history**: Old City has converted into a restaurant and gallery neighborhood. First Friday (the first Friday of each month, galleries open late) is the most active cultural event on the neighborhood calendar. The Market Street corridor has both historic and contemporary buildings.

**For World Cup visitors**: A base here puts you 10 minutes on foot from most historical sites and within walking distance of the Market-Frankford Line (to connect to the Broad Street Line for Gillette... for Lincoln Financial Field). Hotels are boutique rather than chain.

**Stay**: $170–290/night. The **Morris House Hotel** (225 S 8th Street) is the best small boutique hotel — a 1787 Federal townhouse with period details. The **Hotel Monaco** (433 Chestnut Street) is a more standard boutique.

## Center City (Rittenhouse Square)

Rittenhouse Square is a park surrounded by the finest residential and hotel corridor in Philadelphia — the 19th-century brownstones, the Rittenhouse Hotel (one of the most formal hotels in the city), and the restaurant concentration on Walnut Street and Spruce Street.

**What's here**: The Philadelphia Museum of Art (15 minutes walk north), Rittenhouse Square itself (10 acres, active all summer), and the commercial strip on Walnut Street. The Rocky steps at the Art Museum are a Philadelphia cultural reference — the 72 stone steps where Sylvester Stallone's character ran in the 1976 film are in front of the museum.

**Stay**: $190–380/night for the best Rittenhouse hotels. The **Rittenhouse Hotel** (210 W Rittenhouse Square) is the most formal; the **AKA Rittenhouse Square** is better value.

---

## Fishtown and Northern Liberties

Fishtown is Philadelphia's most active neighborhood for food and nightlife — a former working-class fishing neighborhood northeast of Old City that has gentrified into the restaurant corridor for the city's most creative cooking.

**What's there**: Frankford Avenue from Girard Avenue north is the primary commercial strip. Suraya (Lebanese), Wm. Mulherin's Sons (Italian in a converted whiskey factory), Riverwards Produce Market (a small grocery with excellent prepared food), and the coffee shops and bars that have made Fishtown the default destination for Philadelphians who care about food.

**Stay**: Primarily Airbnb ($100–180/night). The **Canopy by Hilton Philadelphia Center City** is the closest hotel; most Fishtown visitors use Airbnb.

---

## South Philadelphia

South Philadelphia is the neighborhood of the Italian-American community, the African American community, and the sports stadiums. It is also where Pat's King of Steaks and Geno's Steaks (the two competing cheesesteak institutions at the corner of 9th and Passyunk) operate.

**9th Street Italian Market**: The Italian Market has operated continuously since 1915 — the southern portion of 9th Street with Italian and now Mexican, Vietnamese, and other immigrant businesses. Open-air stands, indoor specialty stores, cheese shops, and butchers. The best market experience in Philadelphia.

**East Passyunk Avenue**: The diagonal street that runs from South Philly through the residential grid — the most active restaurant corridor in Philadelphia, with Zahav (Israeli, James Beard Award winner), Brigantessa (Italian), and dozens of independent operations.

**Stay**: Limited hotels in South Philly itself; primarily Airbnb ($90–160/night). 15-minute Broad Street Line ride to Center City.

---

## University City (West Philadelphia)

Drexel University and the University of Pennsylvania occupy University City across the Schuylkill River from Center City. The neighborhood has been developing with hotel inventory for visitors to the universities and the hospital complex.

**What's here**: The University of Pennsylvania Museum of Archaeology and Anthropology (one of the great archaeology museums in the US), the Barnes Foundation–adjacent cultural institutions, and the Spruce Street dining corridor.

**Stay**: $150–250/night at the Inn at Penn (the Penn-adjacent hotel) and University City hotels.
