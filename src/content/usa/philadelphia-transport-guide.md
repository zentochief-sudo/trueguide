Philadelphia has a functional transit system built for a city that was more transit-dependent in the 20th century than it is today. For World Cup visitors, the essential systems are two: the **Broad Street Line** (direct subway to [Lincoln Financial](/united-states/lincoln-financial-guide) Field) and the **Market-Frankford Line** (east-west line connecting Old City, Center City, and University City). Together, these cover the primary visitor areas and stadium access.

---

## SEPTA (Southeastern Pennsylvania Transportation Authority)

SEPTA operates Philadelphia's transit network: subway lines, bus routes, regional rail, and the Norristown High Speed Line. For World Cup visitors, two subway lines matter most.

---

## Broad Street Line (BSL)

The Broad Street Line runs north-south under Broad Street — the central north-south axis of Philadelphia's grid. For World Cup purposes, the critical feature is the terminus at **AT&T Station (NRG)** in the South Philadelphia sports complex, adjacent to Lincoln Financial Field.

**Key stations**:
- **City Hall**: Center of the grid; connection point with the Market-Frankford Line.
- **Ellsworth-Federal**: South of Center City; 10 minutes from AT&T.
- **AT&T Station**: Stadium terminus, 3 minutes walk to Lincoln Financial Field.

**Journey times to AT&T Station**:
- From City Hall: 15 minutes
- From Temple University (northern neighborhoods): 25 minutes

**Fare**: $2.50/ride. SEPTA Key card accepted.

**Match day service**: SEPTA operates enhanced BSL service on match days — trains every 3–4 minutes. The line is designed for sports event crowds and handles post-match loads well.

---

## Market-Frankford Line (MFL)

The Market-Frankford Line runs east-west — from Upper Darby on the west through 30th Street (University City), Center City, Old City, and east to Frankford. It is the line for internal city movement.

**Key stations for visitors**:
- **30th Street**: Amtrak station, University City connection
- **15th Street/City Hall**: Interchange with the Broad Street Line (the two lines cross here)
- **8th Street (Old City)**: The Independence Hall area
- **2nd Street**: The eastern edge of Old City, closest to Fishtown

**Fare**: $2.50/ride. Same card as BSL.

---

## Getting to Lincoln Financial Field

**Broad Street Line**: Board at any station heading south. AT&T Station is the southern terminus — you cannot overshoot it. Walk 3 minutes to the stadium from the station.

**From Old City (8th Street MFL station)**: Take MFL westbound to City Hall, transfer to BSL southbound. Total: 20–25 minutes.

**From the airport**: Take SEPTA Airport Line to 30th Street Station, Market-Frankford Line to City Hall, Broad Street Line to AT&T Station. Total: 50–60 minutes.

---

## Philadelphia International Airport (PHL)

Philadelphia International Airport is 8 miles southwest of Center City.

**SEPTA Airport Line**: The airport has a dedicated SEPTA rail line running from the terminals to Center City (30th Street Station, then Market-Frankford Line). Journey: 25–30 minutes. Cost: $6.75 including the connection fare.

**Rideshare**: $20–35 to Center City. Airport traffic can extend this by 15–20 minutes during peak hours.

**No direct SEPTA to the stadium**: From the airport, connect via Center City (see above for the full chain).

---

## Regional Rail

SEPTA Regional Rail connects Philadelphia to the suburbs and to Trenton, NJ (for New York Amtrak connections). For World Cup visitors:

**Amtrak from 30th Street Station**: High-speed Acela and regional Northeast Corridor trains connect Philadelphia to New York (90 minutes, $50–200+ depending on fare class), Washington DC (90 minutes), and Boston (4 hours). Philadelphia's position on the Northeast Corridor makes day trips to New York or Washington feasible.

---

## Walking

Philadelphia's grid is highly walkable. Penn's 1682 plan created a rational system that makes navigation intuitive:
- Center City (from the Delaware River to the Schuylkill River, north to Vine Street, south to South Street) is all walkable within 40 minutes.
- Old City to Rittenhouse Square: 20–25 minute walk.
- Old City to Reading Terminal Market: 10 minutes.
- Reading Terminal to Broad Street Line (City Hall station): 5 minutes.

---

## Practical Notes

**SEPTA Key Card**: Buy at airport machines or Center City stations. $2.50/ride. Also available online for pre-loading before arrival. No card fee.

**Traffic**: Philadelphia has significant traffic on I-95 and the Schuylkill Expressway. Do not drive to the stadium; the Broad Street Line is faster, cheaper, and stress-free.

**Rideshare in Philly**: Uber and Lyft are available citywide. Cross-city trips: $12–22. Post-match surge from the Lincoln Financial area: $25–45.

**Cycling**: Indego bike share (Philadelphia's system) has stations throughout Center City and the neighborhoods. $4/30 minutes or $20/day unlimited. The Schuylkill River Trail runs along the river from Center City to Valley Forge — 25+ miles of off-road trail. Good for exploring beyond the urban core.
