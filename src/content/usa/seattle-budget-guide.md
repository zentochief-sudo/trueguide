Seattle sits in the middle of World Cup city costs — more expensive than Dallas or Houston, less expensive than New York or San Francisco. The tech industry has raised housing and restaurant prices over the past decade, but the combination of excellent transit (Link Light Rail from the airport costs $3.25 and goes directly to the stadium area) and a genuinely accessible seafood market culture (Pike Place Market has world-class food at accessible prices) provides value that the raw numbers don't fully reflect.

---

## Accommodation

**Budget hotels**: $100–160/night. Downtown and Belltown have older properties at lower price points; Capitol Hill has fewer hotels but Airbnb at $90–160/night.

**Mid-range hotels**: $170–280/night. Standard national chains in downtown, Belltown, and the waterfront.

**Boutique**: $200–320/night. The **Inn at the Market** (Pike Place), **Hotel Max** (downtown, music-art theme), and similar properties.

**Airbnb**: Capitol Hill and Fremont: $90–170/night for well-located apartments. Ballard apartments: $100–180/night.

**World Cup premium**: 35–55% above base rates on match weeks. Book 4+ months ahead for viable inventory.

---

## Transport

**Link Light Rail**: $2.25–3.25/ride. Airport to downtown: $3.25. Downtown to stadium: $2.25.

**ORCA Day Pass**: $4.50 (unlimited Link + bus). Worth buying if making 3+ trips.

**Airport rideshare**: $35–55 to downtown (vs. $3.25 by Link). The Link is almost always correct unless traveling with significant luggage.

**Rideshare within the city**: $12–25 for cross-neighborhood trips.

**Rental car**: $45–75/day plus parking ($15–25/day downtown).

---

## Food

Seattle's food costs more than Houston but the quality ceiling is higher and the market-format options (Pike Place) provide excellent value.

**$30–45/day eating well**:
- Breakfast: Pike Place Chowder or crumpets ($8–12)
- Lunch: Ivar's fish and chips or International District pho ($14–16)
- Dinner: Taquería or ramen ($15–18)

**$75–100/day at restaurants**:
- One restaurant dinner ($55–70 at somewhere like The Walrus and the Carpenter) plus casual meals

**The specific value case**: Pike Place Market prepared foods and the International District restaurants offer very good food at $12–16 per meal. The expensive version of Seattle eating happens when you book the farm-to-table restaurants (Sitka & Spruce, The Whale Wins), which are worth it but set a different budget expectation.

## Attractions

**Free**:
- Pike Place Market walk
- The Fremont neighborhood and sculptures
- Discovery Park coastal trail (largest park in Seattle, 534 acres, Pacific views)
- Olympic Sculpture Park (outdoor sculpture collection on the waterfront, Seattle Art Museum extension)
- Alki Beach (West Seattle, Puget Sound views)

**Paid**:
- Space Needle: $37–45 (book online). Worth the view on a clear day — Mount Rainier, the Olympic Mountains, the Cascades all visible at once.
- Chihuly Garden and Glass (adjacent to Space Needle): $32. Dale Chihuly's glass sculpture installations.
- Museum of Pop Culture (MoPOP): $35. Jimi Hendrix collection, rock music history, sci-fi exhibitions.
- Seattle Art Museum: $25.

## Sample Daily Budgets

**Budget traveler ($100–120/day)**:
- Budget hotel or Airbnb (Capitol Hill): $100
- Food: $35 (Pike Place market, International District pho)
- Transport: $5 (ORCA card, 2 Link rides)
- Attraction: $0 (Fremont, waterfront, Olympic Sculpture Park)
- Tips: $7
- **Total: ~$147** — primarily hotel-driven; hostel dorm (~$45) drops this significantly

**Mid-range ($230–270/day)**:
- Mid-range hotel (Belltown): $195
- Food: $75 (one restaurant + casual meals)
- Transport: $20 (Link + rideshare)
- Attraction: $35 (Space Needle or MoPOP)
- Tips: $15
- **Total: ~$340**

**Match-day transport**: $4.50 round-trip by Link Light Rail.
