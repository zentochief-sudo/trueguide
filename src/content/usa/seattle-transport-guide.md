Seattle's transit system is less complex than the SF Bay Area's but more functional than most visitors expect from a mid-size American city. The Link Light Rail runs from Sea-Tac Airport through downtown to Capitol Hill and beyond, with a Stadium Station directly adjacent to Lumen Field. For World Cup visitors, the transit equation is simpler than many other host cities: airport to downtown to stadium all on one rail line.

---

## Link Light Rail

Sound Transit's Link Light Rail is the primary transit system for World Cup visitors. The 1 Line (Central Link) runs from Angle Lake (south of Sea-Tac) through the airport, south Seattle neighborhoods, downtown Seattle, Capitol Hill, the University of Washington, and extends north into the suburbs.

**Key stations for World Cup visitors**:
- **Sea-Tac/Airport**: International terminal access. Journey to downtown: 38 minutes.
- **SODO**: South of downtown, near industrial area.
- **Stadium**: Adjacent to Lumen Field and T-Mobile Park. Journey from Westlake: 5 minutes.
- **Pioneer Square**: 3 minutes from Stadium. Historic district.
- **International District/Chinatown**: 2 minutes from Stadium. Asian food corridor.
- **University Street / Westlake**: Downtown core. Central for most hotel locations.
- **Capitol Hill**: The restaurant and nightlife neighborhood. 8 minutes to Stadium.

**Fare**: $2.25–3.25 depending on distance. Airport to downtown: $3.25. Downtown to Stadium: $2.25.

**ORCA Card**: The regional transit card. Available at station ticket machines, $3 card fee. Works on Link, Seattle buses, and Sound Transit Express buses. Contactless payment also accepted at most validators.

**Frequency**: Every 6–12 minutes during peak hours. Enhanced service on match days.

## Getting to Lumen Field

**From downtown (Westlake)**: Link Light Rail southbound to Stadium Station. 5 minutes, $2.25.

**From Capitol Hill**: Link Light Rail southbound to Stadium Station. 8 minutes, $2.25.

**From Sea-Tac Airport**: Link Light Rail northbound to Stadium Station. 38 minutes (approximately), $3.25. Direct — no transfers.

**Walking from Pioneer Square**: 15–20 minutes south on Occidental Avenue.

**Rideshare**: $12–22 from downtown to the stadium (non-match-day). Post-match surge: $20–45. The Light Rail is objectively better value and faster.

## Sea-Tac International Airport

Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA) is 14 miles south of downtown Seattle. The Link Light Rail connection is direct and reliable — the best airport-to-city connection of any World Cup host with this price point ($3.25).

**Link Light Rail from airport**: Follow signs to the Link station inside the airport. The ticketing machines are at the station. Trains depart every 6–15 minutes. The platform is outside/below the main terminal level.

**Rideshare from airport**: $35–55 to downtown Seattle. The designated pickup area is in the parking garage — walk from the terminal to the garage following signs.

**Taxi**: Available at the terminal; rates roughly $55–75 to downtown.

## Seattle Buses (Metro)

King County Metro operates Seattle's bus network — extensive coverage, but less reliable for non-regular users. The most useful routes for visitors:

**Route 40**: Connects downtown to Fremont and Ballard. The primary bus for visiting Seattle's best restaurant neighborhoods without a car.

**Route 44**: Connects the University District to Ballard via Wallingford. Useful for north Seattle exploration.

**Buses downtown**: The 3rd Avenue transit corridor runs multiple routes through the downtown core. The free Ride Area (within downtown) was eliminated, so all trips require payment.

**Transit apps**: Google Maps, One Bus Away (Seattle-specific, very accurate), and the ORCA app all work for Seattle bus and rail navigation.

---

## Water Taxi

King County Water Taxi operates two routes:
- **West Seattle to downtown** (Pier 50, Coleman Dock): A 10-minute crossing from West Seattle to the downtown waterfront. Not primary World Cup transit but a good way to reach West Seattle's beaches and the Alki Point waterfront.
- **Vashon Island**: Longer ferry to Vashon Island, a rural island in Puget Sound — a half-day trip for visitors interested in PNW island character.

**Fare**: $5.75 each way.

## Rental Cars

A rental car expands access to areas outside the Link Light Rail corridor — Ballard, Fremont, the Eastside (Bellevue, Redmond), and the day trip destinations (Mt. Rainier, the Olympic Peninsula, the Cascades).

**From Sea-Tac**: Rental center accessible via shuttle from the main terminal. All major companies.

**Driving in Seattle**:
- **I-5**: The north-south freeway through the city. Congested 7–9am and 4–7pm.
- **The Battery Street Tunnel** (closed) and the recent SR-99 tunnel replacement under downtown have changed some routing.
- **Hills**: Seattle has significant hills north of downtown (First Hill, Capitol Hill, Queen Anne Hill). Some streets are too steep for casual cycling.
- **Parking**: Downtown garages: $20–35/day. Capitol Hill: $12–20/day. Street parking requires attention to permit zones.

---

## Practical Notes

**ORCA Card**: Buy at Sea-Tac Airport upon arrival. The convenience of not worrying about fare payment on every Link and bus trip is worth the $3 card fee.

**Lyft and Uber**: Both operate throughout Seattle. The rideshare economy is competitive — pricing lower than New York but higher than Dallas or Houston.

**Hills**: Seattle's hills are real. Google Maps routing in "walking" mode accounts for the elevation, but the climb from the Pike Place area up to Capitol Hill (100+ meters elevation gain) is genuinely steep. The Link Light Rail from Westlake to Capitol Hill Station saves this climb entirely.
