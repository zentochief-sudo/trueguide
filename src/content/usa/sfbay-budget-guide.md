San Francisco is one of the three most expensive cities in the United States for accommodation. Combined with Caltrain fares, California restaurant prices, and World Cup demand, the Bay Area is a meaningfully more expensive World Cup destination than Houston, Dallas, or Kansas City. The value case exists in the food (Mission taquerias, Chinatown dim sum, the Ferry Building market) and in the free attractions (Golden Gate Park, the waterfront, the neighborhoods themselves). Understanding where the money goes helps plan against it.

---

## Accommodation

**Budget options**: San Francisco has few genuine budget hotels. Hostels: $55–90/night for a dorm bed. The cheapest private hotel rooms in SF: $140–180/night for older properties in the Tenderloin or SOMA.

**Mid-range hotels**: $200–340/night. Midrange options in SOMA, Union Square, and the Mission. The **Hotel Zephyr** (Fisherman's Wharf, $210–280) and similar properties offer reasonable value for location.

**Oakland alternative**: 30–40% lower than comparable SF properties. Mid-range Oakland hotels: $140–220/night with BART access to SF.

**South Bay / Santa Clara**: $130–240/night in the Silicon Valley hotel corridor — practical for stadium access, less interesting as a base.

**Airbnb**: A one-bedroom in the Mission or SOMA: $150–250/night. San Francisco has strict short-term rental regulations that reduced Airbnb supply — prices are higher than comparable apartments in other cities.

**World Cup premium**: 40–60% above base rates during match weeks. Book 4–5 months ahead for any viable rates.

## Transport

**BART single ride**: $2.50–10 depending on distance. SFO to downtown: $9.65.

**Caltrain to Levi's Stadium**: $8–12 one-way.

**Muni**: $2.50 per ride. Day Pass: $13.

**Ferry**: $8–14 depending on route.

**Clipper Card** (regional pass): $3 card fee, then pay-as-you-go across all systems.

**Rideshare**: Competitive pricing in SF.
- SFO to downtown: $40–65
- Mission to Embarcadero: $12–18
- Post-match surge from Santa Clara: $45–80

## Food

The full spectrum exists in SF — from $10 burritos to $200/person tasting menus.

**$30–45/day eating well**:
- Breakfast: Tartine or bakery ($8–12)
- Lunch: Mission burrito or Chinatown dim sum ($12–16)
- Dinner: Pre-made items from the Ferry Building market or a taqueria ($12–18)

**$90–120/day at restaurants**:
- One restaurant dinner ($55–75) plus casual meals
- Coffee culture adds $6–10/day at SF coffee prices

**The best value meals**:
- Mission taquerias: $10–13 for a burrito that is a full meal
- Chinatown bakeries: $3–5 for pineapple buns and egg tarts
- Ferry Building Saturday market prepared foods: $12–18
- Swan Oyster Depot: Cash only, mid-range seafood in a historic setting

## Attractions

**Free**:
- Golden Gate Bridge walk
- Golden Gate Park (1,017 acres: Japanese Tea Garden, de Young Museum is free on the first Tuesday of the month, Conservatory of Flowers)
- The Embarcadero waterfront
- Coit Tower exterior and neighborhood
- Haight-Ashbury walk
- Lands End coastal trail

**Paid**:
- de Young Museum (Golden Gate Park): $25
- California Academy of Sciences: $40
- Alcatraz: $45 (book 2–3 weeks in advance — sells out)
- SFMOMA: $25

**Day trip costs** (see separate day trips article):
- Napa/Sonoma wine: $50–120 for tasting fees + transport
- Muir Woods: $15 park fee + $35 round-trip ferry to Sausalito
- Monterey/Carmel: $30–50 transport, free coastal access

## Sample Daily Budgets

**Budget traveler ($110–130/day)**:
- Hostel dorm or Oakland Airbnb: $70
- Food: $35 (burrito, dim sum, grocery items)
- Transport: $15 (Clipper Card, BART, Muni)
- Attraction: $0 (Golden Gate Park, Embarcadero)
- Tips: $7
- **Total: ~$127**

**Mid-range ($300–360/day)**:
- Mid-range SF hotel (SOMA): $240
- Food: $75 (one restaurant dinner + casual meals)
- Transport: $25 (Caltrain + BART + Muni)
- Attraction: $25 (museum or Alcatraz)
- Tips: $15
- **Total: ~$380**

**Match-day addition**: $18–24 round-trip Caltrain from SF to Santa Clara. Add to any daily total on match days.
