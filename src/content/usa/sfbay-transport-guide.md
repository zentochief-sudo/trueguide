The SF Bay Area has the most complex transit network of any World Cup host — multiple overlapping systems (BART, Caltrain, Muni, VTA, ferry, AC Transit) each covering different parts of the Bay Area. For World Cup visitors, three systems matter: Caltrain (SF to stadium), BART (SF internal and Oakland), and Muni (within San Francisco). Everything else is useful but secondary.

---

## Caltrain (SF to Levi's Stadium)

Caltrain is the commuter rail connecting San Francisco to San Jose, running down the Peninsula along the western shore of the Bay. It is the primary stadium transit for World Cup visitors based in San Francisco.

**Key stations**:
- **San Francisco 4th & King**: The SF terminus, in SOMA — walkable from most SOMA hotels, accessible via Muni from anywhere in the city.
- **Santa Clara**: The station nearest Levi's Stadium — 15-minute walk or shuttle.

**Journey time**: 55–65 minutes on the Baby Bullet express (limited stops). Local trains take 75–90 minutes.

**Schedule**: Baby Bullet express runs every 30 minutes during peak hours; local service every 15–20 minutes. Match-day service is enhanced with additional trains.

**Fare**: Zone-based. SF to Santa Clara: $8–12 one-way. Buy tickets at station ticket machines or via the Caltrain app. Clipper Card (the Bay Area's regional transit card) works on Caltrain.

**Match day timing**: Depart 4th & King at least 90 minutes before kickoff. Return trains run for 2+ hours after the final whistle.

## BART (Bay Area Rapid Transit)

BART is the regional heavy rail system connecting San Francisco, Oakland, the East Bay, and (via the extension) the South Bay. For World Cup visitors, BART handles:

1. **Airport transit**: SFO Airport to downtown SF — the BART SFO line connects directly to the terminal. Journey time: 30 minutes. Cost: $9.65.
2. **San Francisco internal**: BART runs under Market Street through SF with stations at Embarcadero, Montgomery, Powell, Civic Center, 16th Mission, 24th Mission — covering the major visitor areas.
3. **Oakland access**: 12-minute crossing under the Bay to Oakland. Useful for Oakland-based visitors and the Temescal/Rockridge restaurant neighborhood.

**How to pay**: Clipper Card (contactless payment card, available at all BART stations, $3 initial fee). Also accepts credit/debit contactless at newer fare gates. Single-use paper tickets available.

**Limitations**: BART does not serve the Caltrain 4th & King station directly — a 10-minute Muni ride or 20-minute walk bridges the gap.

## Muni (San Francisco Internal Transit)

The San Francisco Municipal Railway operates buses, light rail (Metro), and the historic cable cars within the city. For World Cup visitors:

**Muni Metro**: Light rail lines running underground through Market Street and surfacing in neighborhoods. Key for getting from BART stations to SOMA/4th & King without walking.

**Buses**: The 49 Van Ness, the 14 Mission, and the 38 Geary serve the main visitor corridors.

**Cable Cars**: Three lines operate on the hills — the Powell-Hyde and Powell-Mason lines run from Powell Street (near Union Square) to Fisherman's Wharf. Primarily a tourist experience ($8 per ride); not an efficient transit method but an iconic SF experience.

**Cost**: $2.50 per ride. Muni Day Pass: $13. Clipper Card works across Muni, BART, and Caltrain.

## Ferry

The San Francisco Bay Ferry system connects the Ferry Building (Embarcadero) to Oakland, Alameda, Sausalito, Vallejo, and other Bay Area points. A practical transit option for visitors staying in Oakland or making a day trip to Sausalito.

**SF to Sausalito**: 30-minute ferry, $14 each way. The ferry view of the Golden Gate Bridge from the Bay is the best available angle. Sausalito has waterfront restaurants, galleries, and the Fish. restaurant.

**SF to Oakland**: 10-minute crossing, $8 each way. Faster than BART for reaching Jack London Square area.

**Schedule**: Ferries run every 30–60 minutes from the Ferry Building. Check schedules at 511.org.

## Airports

### San Francisco International Airport (SFO)

The primary international hub, 14 miles south of downtown SF.

**BART direct**: 30-minute ride, $9.65. Connects directly to all SF BART stations. This is the best airport transit option of any World Cup city — the BART ride from the terminal to Embarcadero BART station is direct, frequent, and reliable.

**Rideshare**: $40–65 to downtown SF. Designated rideshare pickup area in the garage.

**Caltrain from SFO**: Connect at Millbrae station (BART and Caltrain share the station at Millbrae). Adds 30 minutes to a Caltrain journey.

### Oakland International Airport (OAK)

A less busy alternative with lower average fares, particularly on Southwest Airlines and budget carriers.

**BART from OAK**: BART connector bus from the terminal to Coliseum/Airport BART station, then BART into SF. Total: 40–50 minutes to downtown SF. Cost: ~$11.

**Better for Oakland-based visitors**: OAK to Oakland neighborhoods is 20–30 minutes by BART.

### San Jose International Airport (SJC)

Serves the South Bay. VTA bus connection to San Jose Diridon station, then Caltrain north to SF or direct to Santa Clara for the stadium.

## Clipper Card

The Clipper Card is the Bay Area's universal transit payment card — works on BART, Caltrain, Muni, VTA, and the ferry. For visitors spending more than 2 days in the Bay Area, it simplifies all transit payments.

Get it at: BART station ticket machines, Caltrain vending machines, or online (ships within 3–5 days, so only useful if ordered before departure).

**Cost**: $3 card fee. Load value in any denomination. Some transit agencies offer a discount for Clipper payments.

## Getting to the Stadium Summary

| Origin | Method | Time | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| SOMA SF | Walk to 4th & King → Caltrain | 65–75 min total | $10–12 |
| Mission SF | Muni to 4th & King → Caltrain | 75–90 min total | $12–14 |
| Oakland | BART to SF → Muni → Caltrain | 85–100 min total | $16–20 |
| San Jose downtown | VTA light rail from Diridon | 10 min | $2.50 |
| Santa Clara hotels | Walking / rideshare | 10–20 min | $10–25 |

The SOMA-to-Caltrain route is the optimal match-day chain for most visitors.
