SoFi Stadium in Inglewood is the most expensive stadium ever built. Completed in 2020 at a cost of approximately $5.5 billion, it was designed by HKS Architects with a translucent roof membrane, a 70,000-square-foot double-sided video board (called "The Oculus"), and a capacity that expands from its standard 70,240 to approximately 100,000 for the World Cup. It is, by most technical measures, the most sophisticated football stadium in the world.

Understanding how it works before you arrive will save you significant time and frustration.

---

## The Venue

**Address**: 1001 Stadium Drive, Inglewood, CA 90301

SoFi Stadium is part of the **Hollywood Park** development — a 298-acre mixed-use complex that also includes the YouTube Theater (a 6,000-seat concert venue), a casino, a hotel, and the Kia Forum (the former Great Western Forum, a legendary concert venue). The entire complex is designed as a walkable destination, which matters for pre-match planning.

The stadium has a translucent ethylene tetrafluoroethylene (ETFE) roof that covers all seats without fully enclosing the venue — air circulates through open sides. In June and July, this provides shade while allowing the Pacific Ocean breeze to move through. Temperatures inside are typically 2–4°C cooler than the surrounding area.

The Oculus video board is genuinely impressive. At 70,000 square feet and visible from both the east and west sides of the stadium simultaneously, it functions as the primary scoreboard and a massive replay screen. Replays shown on it are detailed enough that fans in the upper tiers can read jersey numbers on close-ups.

## Getting There

**By Metro (recommended)**:
The **K Line (Crenshaw/LAX Line)** has a stop at **Inglewood – Fairview Heights**, approximately 1.5 miles from the stadium. World Cup match day shuttles run from this station to the stadium gates from 2.5 hours before kickoff until 1.5 hours after the final whistle.

Journey times from key neighborhoods:
- From Santa Monica (E Line to K Line transfer): 55–65 minutes
- From Hollywood (B Line to downtown, then E/K Line): 65–80 minutes
- From Koreatown (D Line to Expo/Vermont, then E/K Line): 50–60 minutes
- From Downtown LA: 45–55 minutes

**By rideshare**:
Match-day surge pricing is significant. Budget $60–120 from Hollywood or Santa Monica. Prices are highest 60–90 minutes before kickoff. Post-match rideshare takes 20–40 minutes to arrive due to high demand near the venue.

The designated rideshare pickup/dropoff zone is on the east side of the stadium at Prairie Avenue. Do not expect to be dropped at the main entrance — the stadium perimeter road closes to civilian traffic 3 hours before kickoff.

**By car**:
Parking on the Hollywood Park complex costs $50–80 (pre-purchased) and $80–120+ on the day. The preferred approach from the 405: exit Manchester Avenue, travel west. From the 105: exit Prairie Avenue, travel north.

Post-match, Google Maps will route 30,000 cars simultaneously. The standard exit takes 60–90 minutes. The workaround: stay for 20 minutes after the final whistle (stadium food and drinks continue) and let the first wave clear before walking to your car or rideshare.

## Stadium Arrival

**When to arrive**: FIFA World Cup matches at SoFi will enforce a clear-bag policy and enhanced security screening. Arrive at least 90 minutes before kickoff. The security queue for a sold-out match can take 20–35 minutes.

**Bag policy**: Clear bags only. Single clear plastic bags up to 12" × 6" × 12", or small clutch bags (4.5" × 6.5"). Backpacks are prohibited unless they are clear. Camera bags, diaper bags, and medical bags may be allowed with separate inspection — check the FIFA and SoFi Stadium official websites for the current match-day policy, which may differ from their NFL game policy.

**What to bring**:
- Ticket (digital QR code on your phone, or printed backup)
- TAP card for transit
- ID (required for alcohol purchase — ID must be issued by the country on your passport, or a passport itself)
- Cash is accepted at most stadium vendors; card is faster
- A light layer — the ETFE roof keeps temperatures reasonable but evening matches can get cool

**What not to bring**: Large bags, outside food or beverages (exceptions for sealed water bottles up to 1 liter), professional cameras with lenses longer than 6 inches, noise makers that block views, laser pointers.

## Inside the Stadium

**Seating layout**: SoFi's World Cup configuration places the field of play in the standard orientation, with the sideline seats (Lower Bowl sections 100–160 range) closest to the action. The Club Level (200s) has premium seating with climate-controlled indoor areas. The Upper Bowl (300–500s) has the best views for reading team shape and pressing lines — the elevated angle shows the full pitch clearly.

**The Oculus**: The double-sided video board is suspended above the middle of the field. Every seat in the stadium has a direct sightline to at least one side of the board. The replay quality from the upper sections is excellent.

**Concourse**: SoFi has an unusually good concourse-to-seat sightline design — you can watch play from most concourse areas. This makes food and bathroom trips during live play less painful than most stadiums.

## Food and Drink Inside

SoFi Stadium has invested significantly in its food program beyond the standard stadium fare.

**Standard options**: Hot dogs, nachos, popcorn, burgers ($15–20). Available at every concourse level.

**LA-specific options** that FIFA match-day vendors are expected to include:
- **Tacos and burritos** — the venue has featured local taco vendors at NFL games; World Cup configuration will likely expand this
- **Korean BBQ wraps** — a nod to the LA demographic
- **California-style bowls** (grain-based with grilled protein)

**Alcohol**: Beers start at $15–17 for 16oz. Cocktails run $18–22. The premium clubs have better options at higher prices. FIFA has historically allowed alcohol sales in stadium concourses (though not in seating bowls) — check match-day policy as FIFA rules on this vary by tournament.

**Tips**: The longest queues form at the main concourse vendor rows. The upper bowl concessions are typically 30–40% shorter queues. Buy at halftime or during the first 15 minutes of each half if possible.

## Accessibility

SoFi Stadium has comprehensive accessibility features:
- Accessible entrances at all gate locations
- Elevator access to all levels
- Accessible seating with companion seats throughout
- Sensory-inclusive rooms (quiet rooms away from match noise)
- Accessible parking in designated lots (require pre-purchase)
- Assistive listening devices available at guest services

The main accessible entrance is at the **West Plaza** (off S. Prairie Avenue). Guest services is located at Section 102 on the main concourse level.

## The YouTube Theater and Kia Forum

If you have time before the match, the broader Hollywood Park campus is worth exploring. The **Kia Forum** (formerly The Forum) is a circular arena built in 1967 that was the home of the Showtime Lakers. It now operates as a concert venue. The lobby is occasionally open for pre-match exploration and has historic photos from the Forum's history.

The **YouTube Theater** is adjacent and hosts large-scale events on non-match days.

The **Hollywood Park Casino** and the retail/restaurant area south of the stadium open early — several restaurants here serve pre-match meals at more reasonable prices than stadium food.

## After the Match

**The immediate exit strategy**: The worst move is to try to leave the stadium immediately at the final whistle alongside 100,000 other people. If you have any flexibility, spend 15–20 minutes after the match at a stadium bar or food stand. The concourse clears substantially in that window.

**Metro**: Match-day return service runs enhanced frequencies on the K Line. The Fairview Heights shuttle queue post-match can take 15–25 minutes. Trains running back toward downtown depart frequently. This is consistently faster than rideshare on match days.

**Walking**: If you're ridesharing, consider walking 10–15 minutes north or east from the stadium perimeter before requesting your car. You move outside the surge pricing radius and improve pickup times.

**Stay in Inglewood**: The Hollywood Park complex has restaurants and bars that stay open after matches. **The Banc** and other full-service restaurants here book up on match days, but bar seating is usually available. Waiting an hour here while the traffic clears is a genuine option.
