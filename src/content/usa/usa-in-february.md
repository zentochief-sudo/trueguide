February is the USA's most event-driven winter month — Mardi Gras in New Orleans is the most famous street party in North America, the Super Bowl brings a week of programming to its host city, and the ski season is at its depth. Valentine's Day fills New York, Chicago, and San Francisco hotel rooms. And in the Sonoran and Mojave deserts of the Southwest, the first wildflower signals appear. February rewards specificity: pick the event or the climate, and build around it.

## Weather in February

**New Orleans:** 9°C to 18°C. Cool to mild — Mardi Gras weather is unpredictable (can be rainy, can be warm). A light jacket plus layers covers most February days.

**New York City:** -1°C to 6°C. Still winter. February can deliver the city's heaviest snowstorms (nor'easters).

**Miami / South Florida:** 18°C to 26°C. Peak Florida winter season continues. February is marginally drier than January.

**Mountain West (ski):** Full season. February snowpack typically at its maximum. Park City, Vail, Aspen at peak conditions.

**Scottsdale / Phoenix:** 11°C to 22°C. The Waste Management Phoenix Open golf tournament (one of the most attended sporting events in the USA) runs in February. Spring training begins in Scottsdale in late February.

**Sonoran Desert:** Wildflower early signals in warm February years — the Organ Pipe Cactus National Monument and Ajo area see early blooms.

**Hawaii:** 21°C to 27°C. Humpback whale peak season — the largest concentration of humpbacks in Hawaiian waters is February-March.

## Mardi Gras — New Orleans

Mardi Gras (Fat Tuesday) is the culmination of the Carnival season that began January 6 — the date varies each year but falls between February 3 and March 9 (it's tied to Easter). The two weeks before Fat Tuesday are the peak parade season:

**The Parade Season:**
- **Uptown parades:** The major krewes parade along the St. Charles Avenue streetcar route — Endymion (one of the largest parades in the USA), Bacchus (celebrity monarchs), Orpheus (celebrity co-founded), Zulu (the historically Black krewe with coconut throws), Rex (the oldest and most traditional)
- **Throws:** The catches from parade floats — plastic beads (mass-produced for the occasion), doubloons (krewe coins), stuffed animals, cups, and specialty throws. Zulu coconuts are the most coveted single throw item.
- **French Quarter on Mardi Gras day:** Fat Tuesday in the Quarter — open containers legal, the crowd density extraordinary, Bourbon Street at maximum chaos. Frenchmen Street, a block from the Quarter, has live jazz with somewhat less chaos.

**What's actually happening:**
- **Krewe balls:** Private, ticketed formal events — the real Mardi Gras for New Orleans society. Visitors can attend some through ticket packages.
- **Neighborhood parades:** The smaller neighborhood parades (Treme, Marigny) have more local character and less tourist density
- **Mardi Gras Indians:** The Native American-inspired Black masking tradition — the most visually extraordinary Mardi Gras tradition, seen on Fat Tuesday morning in specific Treme and Central City intersections

**Practical:**
- Book accommodation in New Orleans for Mardi Gras **6–8 months ahead** — the city fills completely
- Prices during peak Mardi Gras weekend (Thursday-Tuesday) are 3–4x normal
- The week before Mardi Gras (Monday-Wednesday) has good parade access at lower prices

## Super Bowl Host City

The Super Bowl rotates cities — the host city runs Super Bowl week with parties, concerts, and events accessible beyond the game itself. The game sells out instantly through NFL lottery/resale at premium prices, but the surrounding week in the host city is a major event programming window. Check the annual host city for Super Bowl LX (February 2026) and beyond.

## Ski Season Peak

February is the premium ski month — maximum snowpack, minimum daylight loss:

**Colorado Presidents' Day Weekend (third Monday, February):** The busiest ski weekend of the year — avoid or book months ahead. Mid-February midweek is the best combination of February powder and manageable resort crowds.

**Steamboat Springs:** The Colorado resort famous for "champagne powder" — the ultra-dry, light snow that makes Colorado skiing distinctive. February is Steamboat's best month.

**Jackson Hole, Wyoming:** The most challenging major ski resort in the USA — 4,139 feet of vertical, intense terrain. February is peak season; the town of Jackson is a genuine western village (elk refuge in the town square).

**Sun Valley, Idaho:** The original destination ski resort (built 1936) — Bald Mountain is one of the best single-mountain ski experiences in the USA. February: Hemingway's ghost, classic lodges, excellent snow.

## Death Valley and Anza-Borrego — Wildflower Watch

**Death Valley National Park:** In wet years, February brings the first wildflower signals to Death Valley — the lowest point in North America in bloom. The phenomenon is called the "superbloom" and occurs 3–5 times per decade in above-average rainfall years. Check the Death Valley wildflower forecast (maintained by National Park Service).

**Anza-Borrego Desert State Park (Southern California):** The largest state park in California — February-March wildflower season in wet years produces fields of desert lilies, brittlebush, and ocotillo. The Salton Sea (a shrinking saline lake below sea level) is accessible from Anza-Borrego.

## Hawaii — Whale Season Peak

February is the best month for humpback whale watching in Hawaii:

**Maui:** The primary humpback wintering ground — the channel between Maui, Lana'i, and Moloka'i (the Au'Au Channel) hosts the largest concentration of North Pacific humpbacks from November-April. February is the peak.

**Whale watches:** Multiple operators run morning whale watches from Lahaina Harbor and Ma'alaea Harbor on Maui. Pacific Whale Foundation runs research-based tours. Whale sightings are essentially guaranteed in February.

**Other February Hawaii:** The Big Island has active lava flow activity (Kilauea), Kauai's Na Pali Coast is accessible by boat (calmer February sea conditions than winter), and O'ahu's North Shore surf season is still running at Banzai Pipeline.

## Practical Notes

- **Mardi Gras safety:** The French Quarter on Fat Tuesday is extremely crowded — pickpockets are common. Keep valuables secure. The parades themselves (along St. Charles) are family-appropriate; Bourbon Street is not.
- **Ski resort Presidents' Day:** Book accommodation for Presidents' Day weekend (third weekend in February) 4–6 months ahead if targeting Colorado or Utah ski resorts.
- **Death Valley superbloom:** Only occurs in above-average rainfall years — check the NPS wildflower hotline before making the trip specifically for blooms.

## The Short Version

February USA is defined by Mardi Gras — the most famous popular festival in North America, with two weeks of parade season building to Fat Tuesday. Ski season is at peak snowpack and peak prices across the Mountain West. Miami and Hawaii deliver the best winter sun options. And the desert Southwest, in favorable rainfall years, begins its wildflower show. February rewards advance planning more than any other winter month — the major events book far ahead.
