FIFA World Cup 2026 tickets are available through the official FIFA+ Ticketing platform at [tickets.fifa.com](https://tickets.fifa.com). This guide covers prices, categories, how to navigate the system, and what to do if you're looking for tickets now that the tournament has started.

## Official ticket platform: FIFA+ Ticketing

**URL**: [tickets.fifa.com](https://tickets.fifa.com)

FIFA+ Ticketing is the only official source for World Cup tickets. All tickets are digital and tied to a FIFA account. Creating an account is free and required before purchasing.

**How to buy**:
1. Create a FIFA+ account at [fifa.com/en/fifaplus](https://www.fifa.com/en/fifaplus)
2. Go to [tickets.fifa.com](https://tickets.fifa.com)
3. Select your match, category, and quantity
4. Complete payment (credit card, PayPal accepted)
5. Tickets appear in your FIFA+ account — show from the app or print

**Important**: Tickets are non-transferable in the official system. Each ticket is linked to the buyer's ID. Stadium entry requires the ticketholder to show their ID.

## Are tickets still available?

Group stage tickets for popular matches (Mexico, Brazil, Argentina games) sold out in the first sales windows. However:

**Check the official platform first**: FIFA releases returned or reallocated tickets in batches throughout the tournament. Checking [tickets.fifa.com](https://tickets.fifa.com) daily (or multiple times per day) during the tournament often yields available tickets, especially for less high-profile group stage matches.

**Knockout round tickets**: Round of 32 and Round of 16 tickets are partially available closer to the specific matches being determined, since the specific teams involved aren't known until the group stage resolves. Some of these become available after group stage results confirm.

## Secondary market options

If official tickets are sold out, secondary market platforms have inventory. Be prepared to pay 2–5x the face value for in-demand matches.

### Reputable secondary market platforms

**[StubHub](https://www.stubhub.com)**: The largest secondary ticket market in the US. FIFA World Cup 2026 section has active listings. StubHub offers a buyer guarantee — if tickets are invalid, they replace them or refund. Use the filter to sort by price and seat location.

**[Viagogo](https://www.viagogo.com)**: International platform, strong inventory for Mexico and Canada matches. Pricing can be aggressive; compare with StubHub before buying.

**[Vivid Seats](https://www.vividseats.com)**: US-focused, competitive pricing on domestic markets (Dallas, NYC, LA). Rewards program for regular buyers.

**[SeatGeek](https://www.seatgeek.com)**: Aggregates listings from multiple sellers. The "Deal Score" feature helps identify relative value. Good for quick price comparisons.

### Secondary market pricing guide

Current secondary market prices (as of tournament start) for group stage matches involving top teams:

- **Mexico group matches**: $250–650 (massive demand from Mexican fans)
- **Brazil/Argentina group matches**: $180–450
- **USA group matches**: $120–300
- **European powerhouse group matches**: $100–250
- **Smaller nations group matches**: $60–150 (often below face value)

Knockout rounds: prices increase 30–60% per round from these baselines.

## How to avoid scams

The World Cup secondary market attracts fraud. Protect yourself:

1. **Never buy from individuals on social media or Craigslist**. Fake ticket scams are extremely common at major events.
2. **Use established platforms only** (StubHub, Viagogo, Vivid Seats, SeatGeek). They have buyer protection.
3. **Verify the ticket format**: FIFA tickets are digital and tied to the buyer's ID. Paper tickets sold as "official" by individuals are likely counterfeit.
4. **Check reviews of the seller** on secondary platforms before buying.
5. **If a deal looks too good, it's probably a scam**. Quarter-final tickets for $50 on Facebook are not real.

## At the stadium without a ticket

Even without a match ticket, the World Cup experience in host cities is substantial:

**FIFA Fan Zones**: Free entry. Large outdoor venues with live match broadcasts, entertainment, food vendors, and fan activations. Every host city has an official FIFA Fan Zone. They generate the street-level World Cup atmosphere.

**Host city neighborhoods**: On match days, the areas around stadiums and city centers fill with fans from every nation. Bar screenings, public plazas broadcasting matches, street food, and the general carnival atmosphere are free.

**Watch parties**: Every sports bar in every host city is showing every match. Arriving early (1–2 hours before kick-off) secures a spot. The atmosphere at a bar packed with fans from rival nations watching a game live is genuinely electric.

## Tips for getting the most from your tickets

**Buy Category 3 for group stage, Category 1 for knockout rounds**: The difference between Category 3 and Category 1 at a group stage match is real but manageable — you're still at a World Cup match, the atmosphere is the same. For the quarter-finals or semi-finals, the investment in better seating pays off because you'll remember this match for decades.

**Attend multiple group stage matches in one city**: Each host city has 4–8 matches across the group stage. Buying two or three tickets in the same city over consecutive days is more economical and logistics-friendly than flying between cities for individual matches.

**Combine a match ticket with Fan Zone access**: Plan the day around both — Fan Zone in the afternoon, match in the evening, Fan Zone again after the match. This maximizes the World Cup atmosphere per day.

**Book accommodation well before the match date**: Hotel prices surge in the final days before a sold-out match. If you have tickets and haven't booked accommodation, do it immediately.
